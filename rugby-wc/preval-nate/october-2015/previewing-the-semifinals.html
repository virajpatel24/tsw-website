2015 Rugby World Cup - Previewing the Semifinals

South Africa vs. New Zealand; Argentina vs. Australia

by Nate Preval

Share Tweet

This past weekend, the world saw the underdog Bleus receive the routing that New Zealand was long overdue in delivering in this World Cup tournament. Fueled by their loss four years prior, the All Blacks came out with unrelenting pace and it resulted in Nehe Milner-Skudder converting his second hat-trick of the tournament, an enormous accomplishment for someone just 24 years of age. France did play with some tenacity, but it was not nearly enough to slow down the tsunami-like waves of strong Kiwi attack which included forwards lining up deep in the back line and making crisp passes to their fast countrymen. France literally had ZERO chance.

Fortunately for the love of competition, New Zealand will have a more formidable opponent in South Africa, who barely held on against Wales. The Boks were able to seal the deal late thanks to the Herculean efforts of their captain, Fourie du Preez, who managed to get past an overload caused by Duane Vermuelen and followed up with an offload put on a platter for the go-ahead try. It was easily the play of the match and, accordingly, Vermuelen earned the man of the match honors for his performance.

However, this weekend will not be so joyous for the Boks. They have not addressed their issues in the pack thus far and besides the efforts of du Preez and their wingers in the back line, they have yet to assert their dominance in the tournament against formidable opponents. Their ability to also make costly mistakes is still a very real threat and against the All Blacks, they will have to minimize these mistakes in order to win.

My Prediction? New Zealand will take the match and head back to the World Cup Final winning by at least 7 points.

After Ireland’s squad had been gutted with injuries over the course of the tournament, they found themselves unlucky in their match against Argentina. The Pumas, who have a lot rolling for them, truly make a case for including the Americas in more competitive international play each year apart from just the World Cup. They are proving their value in the tournament and that very value allowed them to defeat the mean green Irish machine. It was not a blowout victory, but after the half, it was quite obvious that Ireland had made amends with their fate. The Pumas exploited Ireland’s discomfort in the set piece and attacked them relentlessly with levels in the backline and incorporating their pack in ever rolling free play.

Before Scotland was brutally robbed of a semi-final chance in the tournament, this match was pound-for-pound, hit-for-hit the most entertaining in the entire tournament. The game was ultimately decided by a single point, which resulted from a completely wrong and obviously missed call by the match official. According to the reasoning, there was a knock-on that was then recovered in an offside position, resulting in the end of the game because it was a turnover. However, the rules would state that the official should go back to the original offense, which would have fell in Scotland’s favor, who was in striking distance of both a drop goal or a kick to the corner and maul. All in all, it was just a crushing blow to a perennially powerful Scotland side that truly left everything they had on the pitch.

Fortunately for those who believe in karma, that match official has been heavily scrutinized and will not be chosen as an official for the upcoming semi-final matches this weekend, as expected in this controversial atmosphere. That was an opportunistic quarter-finals win for the Wallabies in a match that they should have lost. Now can they capitalize and try their luck some more against a strong and fit Puma side that has its eyes on getting to the big dance next weekend.

My prediction? Australia will struggle against the Argentinian scrum but make up for it in relentless back line play. However, that probably won’t be enough. Their efforts will be extremely entertaining to watch and the result will be well-earned. Argentina will win by 7.

It should be a high-energy, tightly-contested semi-finals! But only time will tell.

Play on!
