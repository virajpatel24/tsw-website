English Premier League Recap - Matchday 23

Arsenal suffer the Sunday blues as Chelsea hand the Gunners their fifth loss of the season

by Viraj Patel

Share Tweet

The recently-leaked transfer documents seem to have brought forth some juicy tidbits for football fans across the world. Having heard inklings about a possible Gareth Bale transfer, Spurs fans eagerly asked, “Will Bale really return to White Hart Lane?!?” In turn, while Spurs fans salivated over the lethal Kane-Bale partnership, Real Madrid fans openly wondered whether all eight of Cristiano’s abs on the recent GQ cover were Photoshopped or not, with nary a thought about Bale leaving.

Of course, as an Arsenal supporter, I knew Professeur Wenger had dotted every “i” and crossed every “t” such that there wouldn’t be any backlash or scrutiny from these leaked documents. However, according to The Guardian, Mesut Özil’s Arsenal transfer was actually expertly negotiated by Florentino Pérez and Real Madrid. The leaked transfer documents show that if Arsenal ever look to transfer the 27-year-old German maestro back to Spain, then Real Madrid would have first rights in matching any deal that Arsenal accepts. Fortunately, as forward-thinking as Real Madrid might have been in preventing Barcelona from nabbing the playmaker, it seems that the creative midfielder will be able to ply his trade in North London for as long as he wishes. Besides, if those league-leading 16 assists don’t grant you transfer immunity forever, then what exactly does?

For the answer to that question, we turn to beleaguered Chelsea midfielder Cesc Fàbregas. After providing a league-leading 18 assists in the 2014–15 campaign, the Spanish international began turning in abysmal performances in the opening months of this season as he struggled to perform under the watchful eye of former manager José Mourinho. As 2015 drew to a close, it appeared as if Fàbregas’s Chelsea career would do the same, considering that both Juventus and Inter Milan were looking whisk the midfielder away from West London.

But in the greatest of surprises, it seems as if Mourinho’s departure has allowed Cesc’s ethereal vision to fully return. And his knack for threading perfectly weighted through balls has also undergone a roaring return since his Man-of-the-Match performance last weekend against Everton. Having assisted and scored against the Toffees, Fàbregas was just one of the long list of Chelsea players who were eager to make an impact at the Emirates this past Sunday evening. With the likes of Oscar, Eden Hazard, Nemanja Matić, John Terry, and Diego Costa rediscovering their form heading into the Arsenal tie, it only seemed proper that the slightly dipping Gunners would run into some difficult times.

Coming into the match with seven goals, three assists, and fifty fights, Diego Costa was all too prepared to throw it down with Arsenal’s Angel Gabriel. But in anticipation of such a clash, Wenger decided to leave Gabriel on the bench and, instead, opted for the reliable defensive partnership of Per Mertesacker and Laurent Koscielny. And it only took 18 minutes before the reliability disappeared without a moment’s hesitation as the lumbering Mertesacker launched into a lunging challenge that missed both the ball and Diego Costa, who had acres of open field in front of him had Mertesacker not intervened. Costa, whose internal measure of play-acting and flopping hiked up to “Why always me?” levels shortly after the Mertesacker challenge, was instrumental in executing the perfect stop-drop-and-roll.

Of course, the sheer recklessness of the feckless Mertesacker challenge was enough on its own for the referee on hand, Mark Clattenburg, to show the German the straight red. Bedlam ensued as Arsenal dropped to ten men and Wenger pulled the head-scratching substitution of the season by bringing on Gabriel in the place of Olivier Giroud. What made the decision even more unfathomable was the fact that Wenger decided upon it within mere seconds of Mertesacker delivering his lunging challenge. Had Le Professeur waited a few minutes and let the match resume once more before pulling Giroud, he would have been able to verify his reasoning: That Mertesacker’s departure would cause holes in the Arsenal defense, which meant Gabriel had to come on.

While Gabriel’s appearance helped restabilize the Gunners defense, having him replace Giroud meant Arsenal had no reliable figure that would convert on Joel Campbell’s through balls or on Mesut Ozil’s perfect crosses or on Aaron Ramsey’s sinewy runs. The Gabriel-Giroud substitution effectively drew the curtain on Arsenal’s hopes of challenging Thibaut Courtois’s goal. Although Petr Čech worked tirelessly to ward off each and every Chelsea shot, his otherworldly talents were still not enough as Diego Costa found back of the net eventually.

The Gunners should be grateful that the score didn’t read 2–0 or 3–0 as the final whistle echoed throughout the Emirates. Koscielny, who went as far as describing himself as one of the Premiership’s best defenders pre-match, almost gave away a needless penalty in the second half when he decided to bodycheck Fàbregas as the Spaniard wove into the penalty area.

Did Arsenal deserve to lose? At some levels, yes. Mertesacker decided his own fate and hurt that of his team’s with his reckless challenge. The Gabriel-Giroud substitution reeked of a rushed decision on Wenger’s part. And the side just didn’t have enough ammo remaining to contend on the offensive end while being down a man.

Did Chelsea deserve the win? As much as it pains me to admit this, yes. Guus Hiddink’s men retained possession (52% to Arsenal’s 48%) and their passing was clearly superior to that of Arsenal’s. While Arsenal squandered their lone on-target shot, Chelsea created five on-target shots, which allowed for Costa to finally bag the deciding goal. The Blues were also far more aggressive in their offensive pursuits, garnering a remarkable seven offsides calls (compared to Arsenal’s single offsides call). In the end, this 1–0 victory may finally provide what the first meeting — which Chelsea won 2–0 at Stamford Bridge last October — did not offer: A clean slate and a chance to construct a winning streak.
