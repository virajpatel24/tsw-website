English Premier League Recap - Matchday 13

Louis van Gaal’s Manchester United escape Vicarage Road with the 2–1 heist

by Viraj Patel

Share Tweet

Lacking the talents of Wayne “Captain Wazza” Rooney and Anthony Martial due to injuries (an illness for Wazza; a foot injury for Martial), Manchester United manager Louis van Gaal was forced to turn to his midfielder-turned-striker Marouane Fellaini in the hopes of finding a last-minute replacement in this past weekend's tie against a struggling Watford side at Vicarage Road. Fellaini, who declined LvG’s offer with a “no-go, bro” shake of his afro, was also subsequently ruled out. This left Mr. van Gaal with little to no options (I mean, come on, the man had players with the names of “Goss” and “Rashford” on his bench going into this game), which meant Memphis Depay got the surprising start as the team’s lone striker.

Of course, Memphis “give him a year to adapt” Depay hadn’t even sniffed the starting XI these past few weeks due to both his lackluster form and an unlucky patch of injuries. Unfortunately, Memphis had heard the rather loud comparisons of his woeful form being described as “the poor man’s James Wilson,” which, coincidentally, proved to be exactly what the Dutch doctor ordered, considering how hellbent Depay was on proving his worth and value against Watford.

Depay began the Watford game with a sprightly start. His constant movement and rotations, along with those of Juan Mata, Ander Herrera, and Jesse Lingard, gave Watford’s defense nightmares in the opening minutes of the match. Ashley Young took a brilliant run deep into Watford’s right flank before passing it off, as if on cue, to Ander Herrera, who was lurking nearby. Quickly sensing the Lingard-Depay presence near the Watford goal, Herrera launched a cross that would’ve made David Beckham happy from the right flank right onto Depay’s left boot, resulting in the Red Devils’ opening goal.

The rest of the match unfolded with similar eye-popping movement from the Depay-Lingard-Mata-Herrara foursome. Odion Ighalo had Watford’s only chance of the first half as Morgan Schneiderlin and Bastian Schweinsteiger did a brilliant job of keeping the Hornets’ attacks at bay. Heading into half-time, even United’s passing and attack, which had begun with such unbelievable promise, had resumed normal (boring) activities.

The relative calm of the first half and the first twenty-five minutes of the second half were no match for the utter chaos and calamity that ensued in the final twenty minutes. Marcos Rojo, who had clearly had enough of the snoozefest, decided to suddenly snap out of his mid-game nap by bringing down the aforementioned Ighalo in the penalty area. The referee on hand, Robert Madley, decided to award the pivotal penalty to Watford as the 20,000+ capacity crowd jubilantly erupted at Vicarage.

Facing his first real challenge of the match, David de Gea, who had already whisked away Troy Deeney’s half-volley challenge in the 70th minute, nearly blocked the Watford striker’s firecracker of a penalty shot in the 87th. Unfortunately for United and fortunately for Watford, the Hornets were able to level it up at 1–1 with only a dearth of time remaining on the clock.

No stranger to engineering last-second heroics, Bastian Schweinsteiger came through for United as the time hit the 90:00 mark. The opportunistic German latched onto a Lingard shot attempt that was parried away across the face of the Watford goal by keeper Heurelho Gomes. Although the resulting Schweinsteiger pull-back shot attempt had no chance of sniffing the goal, Troy Deeney (yes, the same Deeney that had rescued Watford only minutes before) helped out by accidentally blocking Schweini’s shot right into his own goal.

In the narrowest of margins and the widest of borings, Lord Louis’s United side was able to hightail it out of Hertfordshire with all three points thanks to a last second miracle. Such beautiful endings were not in the making for their cross-town rivals Manchester City, who disappointed in a harrowing 4–1 defeat to Jürgen Klopp’s Liverpool side.

Thankfully, LvG will have his team captain (Rooney) and sensational youngster (Martial) back as his side prepare to take on PSV Eindhoven at Old Trafford tonight in the Champions League. But the storyline grabbing everyone’s attention concerns the woebegone Memphis Depay and whether or not van Gaal deems him worthy of a Champions League start in a tightly-contested Group B.

Presumably, starting against his former club may provide the necessary fuel Depay needs to course-correct his United career. But the start will come with its fair share of pressure and drama, which can potentially derail the tiny triumph that Depay achieved with that scintillating goal against Watford. Then again, situations like these are why United splurged the £19 million for Memphis, right?

Time to rise and shine, Manchester United’s number 7.
