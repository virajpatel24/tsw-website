English Premier League Recap - International Break 1

While the first four match weekends of the Premier League have gone by in a breeze, it’s important to pause and take notice of how far we’ve come.

by Viraj Patel

Share Tweet

The 2015–2016 edition of the English Premier League is already 10.5263% over. To those of us living in normal human years, it only seems like it was yesterday when the Gunners were lifting yet another trophy, after winning the FA Community Shield against Chelsea to fully welcome the new Premiership season. But for one certain individual, these first four games must have dragged perpetually onwards, as if life suddenly chose to pass by in bowhead whale years.

Of course, that turmoiled soul is Chelsea manager José Mourinho, who now consistently carries heavy bags under his eyes and an even heavier burden on his Portuguese shoulders given that his promising Chelsea squad has gotten off to a turbulent start. Chelsea currently sit in 13th-place in the EPL table, just a hair above powerhouse sides like Watford, Sunderland, and Newcastle. First question: How many years has Mourinho aged in this four-match Chelsea slide?

Even more surprising than Chelsea’s unfathomable appearance in the bottom half of the EPL table is the fact that the side’s superstar players, like Eden Hazard, Diego Costa, Pedro, Oscar, Cesc Fàbregas, John Terry, Nemanja Matić, among many others — have made enough sloppy gaffes, defensive errors, offensive mistakes, and mental checkouts throughout the first four games that even the spend-happy Louis van Gaal would easily refrain from submitting a hasty £50 million transfer request in exchange for Radamel Falcao’s services. Second question: Where does Falcao rank on all-time stumbles for a superstar of a player? Is he “Fernando Torres at Chelsea” levels yet??

While we can be certain that the Special One, during this international break, will be channeling all of his years of knowledge and acumen to jolt Chelsea awake from their four match nap, such certainty cannot be applied to Meneer Louis van Gaal, who finds his Manchester United squad nestled comfortably in fifth position in the EPL table. Certainly, van Gaal has continued to solve the evolving jigsaw puzzle that is the United starting eleven. However, the club’s off-the-field drama resulting from the botched David de Gea transfer to Real Madrid on deadline day has cast ominous tones about Lord Louis’s control over his Red Devil domain. Third question(s): Can de Gea really do a 180° turn and sign a long-term extension with United? And, more importantly, will United overpay for him the way they did for Martial? Finally, why does no one ever bring up the young Bayer Leverkusen goalie Bernd Leno, who can, I might add, easily and capably (and cheaply) replace any EPL goalkeeper???

Complicating matters even further has been the unexpected, big-money signing of 19-year-old French striker Anthony Martial for 50 million euros (a figure that can increase even further in coming years if Martial progresses as expected and, as a result, activates the bonus payouts of his transfer). It’s largely an accepted fact in the footballing world that United have inordinately overpaid for the young, promising Martial and, if things go south for United this season, it will be no surprise to anyone when Louis van Gaal arrives at the unemployment office.

While the red half of Manchester continues to figure things out, the blue half of Manchester has gotten off to a firecracker of a start. Manuel Pellegrini’s men have obtained all twelve points while scoring ten goals and preserving four straight clean sheets. Yaya “I’m back!” Touré and Vincent “I’m back, too!!” Kompany have combined to serve as the midfield-defensive spine City needed in order to truly allow their free-flowing offense, led by the triumvirate consisting of the always magical David Silva, the speedy Raheem Sterling, and the Argentine talisman Sergio Agüero, to thunder ahead at full pace.

In fact, when the Premier League reignites its engine once more this upcoming weekend, the top-ranked Manchester City side will play hosts to a rejuvenated second-place Crystal Palace side led by Alan Pardew. While Pardew’s former club, Newcastle, is left to lick its wounds in the bottom of the EPL table, his current club, Crystal Palace, is the one dishing out the lickings on top-tier clubs like Chelsea.

Garry Monk’s Swansea City side have also laid out withering punishments to dithering sides like United and Chelsea in their first four matches of the Premiership season. Even Leicester City is getting in on the act!

Final question: But, the one club that isn’t?

Tottenham Hotspur.

Some things just never change, do they?
