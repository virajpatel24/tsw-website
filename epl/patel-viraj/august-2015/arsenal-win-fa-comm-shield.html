FA Community Shield Woes for José Mourinho and Chelsea F.C.

Arsenal comes away with the 1–0 victory over their rivals Chelsea

by Viraj Patel

Share Tweet

One of the lasting images of Arsenal’s 1–0 victory over their bitter intra-London rivals Chelsea featured a certain self-professed ‘Special One’ who, in one unshaken hand, stole many of the post-match discussions. The heated tensions between Chelsea manager, José ‘Special One’ Mourinho, and Arsenal’s tactician and head honcho, Arsène Wenger, came to the forefront long after the final whistle had signified the end of the FA Community Shield match held on Sunday, August 2nd.

The FA Community Shield is a charity match that pairs the previous season’s Premier League champion (Mourinho’s Chelsea) versus the incumbent FA Cup champion (Wenger’s Arsenal). The proceeds from the match go to community-based initiatives and charities across England. And every year, the match serves as the harbinger of the new Premier League season, which will begin in full force this weekend.

Every year, before the hustle-and-bustle of the Premiership season begins in earnest, the FA Community Shield simultaneously allows the EPL fans to warm up their vocal cords and gives the Tottenham Hotspur fans glimmers of false hope of future glories. This is why the joint Mourinho-Wenger West End production of The Unshaken Hand that took place post-match right on the historic Wembley pitch left both the fans and media alike bewildered and divided.

But, all of this brouhaha over the frosty José-Arsène relationship came at the tail end of a rather listless Chelsea performance versus Arsenal. Both teams had very few chances on goal, but it was Arsenal’s starlet Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain who slotted in the picture-perfect winning goal in the 24th minute. From that point onwards, Arsenal played a stolid defensive line that effectively neutralized Chelsea’s heavy offensive attack featuring former Gunner Cesc Fàbregas, Eden Hazard, Willian, and Radamel Falcao.

Falcao, who completed a one-year loan deal to Chelsea a few weeks ago after a disappointing loan spell with Manchester United last season, did not do much to convince his new Chelsea supporters that he would be returning to the preternatural form he displayed during his Atlético Madrid days. Throughout the match, both Falcao and Loïc Rémy were engaged in a heated battle to score as many offside calls as possible from the referees on hand. If soccer matches were won by the amount of offside calls a team garners, Chelsea would have cruised to a landslide 89–5 victory over their rivals.

“Arsenal ended last season with 20 wins from 27 matches in all competitions, a win ratio of 74% compared to Chelsea’s 58%, and continued where they left off on Sunday.”

— Andy Cryer, BBC Sport

Chelsea’s other stars Hazard and Fàbregas could not do nearly enough to catapult the Blues past the Franco-German alliance of Laurent Koscielny and Per Mertesacker, who were superbly tag-teaming the shepherding of Arsenal’s defense amidst the late Chelsea surges.

But, most importantly, Arsenal’s new Goalkeeper, Petr Čech, who joined Arsenal on a £11 million ($17.2 million) transfer after spending a largely successful eleven seasons at Stamford Bridge, remained relatively unchallenged throughout the match. Nearly sixty-eight minutes went by until Čech was finally tested by the Brazilian Oscar, who produced a sweeping free kick that curved over the Arsenal wall while dipping into the outstretched gloves of a Čech in full-dive. The newly-arrived Čech’s performances for the Gunners have been met with a warm enthusiasm by Arsenal fans thus far. And understandably so, given they had to suffer through the post-game smoking-in-the-team-showers antics of Wojciech Szczesny last season.

When Čech’s former boss, Mourinho, was not busy vilifying the quality of the “slow” Wembley Stadium pitch or condemning the weather in his post-match press conference, he did give credit where credit is due and commended the Gunners’ on achieving a hard-fought clean-sheet. But, Mourinho also attributed Chelsea’s sluggish performance to an expected “lack of sharpness.” Chelsea played the Community Shield match only a few days after arriving back in England from a busy summer U.S. pre-season tour, so jet lag may have contributed to Chelsea’s lackluster performance at Wembley Sunday afternoon.

But, there are still many positives for Mourinho to take away. His side did not field the mercurial Spanish talisman Diego Costa in Sunday’s Community Shield. Just last season, Costa affirmed his sky-high potential by netting 20 goals while only playing in 26 league matches due to injuries. During the Community Shield match on Sunday, Costa, seated on the Chelsea bench, was seen shielding himself from the overbearing sunlight at various times. Hopefully for Mourinho’s Chelsea, Diego will be ready to return to his superb form of last season this weekend as the Premier League kicks off a new campaign.

And, it’s a guarantee that he will have to square off against much brighter opponents that the mid-afternoon London sun.
