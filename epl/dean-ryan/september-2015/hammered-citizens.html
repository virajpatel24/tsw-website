Hammered Citizens

A home loss to Juventus in the Champions League midweek for Man City is followed by another defeat at the Etihad, this time to West Ham.

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

Not quite déjà vu for Manchester City. Just a few days removed from that shocking defeat to Italian Serie A side Juventus, the Citizens have now lost another match. Same scoreline, same venue, different competition. Different level of competition may be more apt, if one aims to compare Juventus to West Ham United. Still, the end result is a stunning 2-1 loss for City, effectively breaking their perfect record in the English Premier League. In addition, the Citizens, with the Juve and West Ham losses, capitulated on their streak of five consecutive matches without conceding a single goal.

All good things must eventually come to an end. In what was a roaring start to the Premier League campaign for City, it was only a matter of when, not if, City would face their first bump on the road to the EPL title. It turns out that that bump arrived in the form of Slaven Bilić’s resurgent West Ham side. To make matters worse, the loss against West Ham immediately followed that heartbreaking midweek defeat to Juventus. After being given somewhat of a lesson by Juventus on Champions League night - at home nonetheless - City was looking for a comfortable shift against the Hammers at the Etihad. It was set to be a fitting stage for City to rebound and avenge their midweek defeat at home. However, it was not meant to be.

It wasn’t for a lack of trying, though. While the scoreline read 2-1 for West Ham, City wasn’t playing the worst football on the pitch. Statistics show that City had a total of 27 shots on goal compared to 6 for West Ham. City also hogged the ball with 69.5% of the possession compared to West Ham’s 30.5%. These metrics clearly indicate City’s intent to take all three points. So, what exactly went wrong for Manuel Pellegrini’s men?

Taking a look at the number of shots taken, the problem seems to be converting chances into goals. In this aspect, there is no one else more culpable than Sergio Agüero. The Argentinian striker has been on a wane lately, unable to convert since the game against Chelsea a number of weeks ago. Now that City has started losing games, his performances will be further scrutinized and questioned. What is even more worrying for City is that his backup, Wilfried Bony, isn’t all that threatening either. Instead of having one striker woefully misfiring, City now have two off-form strikers to contend with.

This scenario is nothing short of a mini-disaster for City considering the wealth of talent in place behind the strikers. Midfielders David Silva, Kevin de Bruyne, Yaya Touré, and company offer an irresistible supply line for the strikers to feed off of. While the midfielders may chip in a few goals every now and then, it falls upon the strikers to shoulder the brunt of the primary goalscoring responsibilities for the team. Clearly, in these past few weeks, the strikers aren’t striking enough.

Another explanation of what went wrong at the Etihad on Saturday is that City simply faced a very effective West Ham side. This incarnation of West Ham, led by their former player Slaven Bilić, has proven to be very effective in their counter-attacks, especially when facing top-tier clubs like City. The warning signs were evident if one was prepared to see them. The Hammers have beaten Arsenal and Liverpool already this season. Even more impressive is that West Ham accomplished those feats while playing away at the Emirates and at Anfield, respectively.

Central to these victories has been Dimitri Payet, who has been instrumental to this new West Ham system. One can only wonder how a player of Payet’s caliber ended up at West Ham instead of other big clubs. One thing is for sure, though: He is relishing his role in this particular West Ham side. In the match against City, he proved decisive once again, this time providing the assist for Victor Moses’ winning goal.

While being effective with their limited chances in front of goal proved to be key in the pivotal victory over City, it is West Ham’s backline that ensured that all three points ended up safely in the bag. Led by the imperious Winston Reid, West Ham’s defense was able to weather the barrage of City attacks in the second half. One could say City threw everything but the kitchen sink at them in search of the equalizer and, perhaps, a winner. However, Adrian’s heroics in the West Ham goal only added to City’s frustration in their frantic search, thus condemning City to their first ever defeat in this season’s Premier League campaign.

Despite two losses in a row at home for City, it is hardly a crisis situation for them. They’re still on top in the Premier League and have five more matches to right the ship in the Champions League. They possess the class and talent needed to overcome this minor bump in the road, as evidenced in some of the players’ performances in the two losses. Newcomers Kevin de Bruyne and Nicolás Otamendi are settling in well and should be a shoo-in for the starting eleven the next time City are in competitive action. Both will represent a welcome headache for Manuel Pellegrini. The Fernandinho and Yaya Toure partnership has been solid as a rock in the middle of the park, providing a great platform for both attack and defense to propagate. They are also the most consistent performers so far for City, something that Pellegrini will hope rub off on the other players, especially Sergio Agüero.

But the best part of it is that, amidst all the gloom and doom, City still sit clear of their inner-city rivals Manchester United in the table. And in a week that featured some tough, unexpected losses, City chief Manuel Pellegrini will appreciate that particular silver lining.
