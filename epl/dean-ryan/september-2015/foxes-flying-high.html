Foxes Flying High

An unfathomable come-from-behind victory from Leicester against Aston Villa ensured the Foxes’ steadfast hold on 2nd place in the English Premier League.

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

It’s funny: Whenever Leicester City is mentioned to me, an image of striker Emile Heskey kicking the ball out of the stadium immediately comes to mind. I wish I knew why. But that’s the image that keeps popping up in my mind. At the time, the home of Leicester City was Filbert Street, before their move to the state-of-the-art King Power Stadium in 2002. Unfortunately, after no sooner than two years at the the new venue, they were relegated. For ten years they languished in the second tier of English football, until their roaring return to the top flight in the 2014-15 season.

It was an exciting time for me. Not because I was an avid supporter of the club or anything. Maybe, it was the manner in which they earned their promotion. The club recorded a stunning 102 points en route to their promotion to the Premier League. To put their accomplishment in perspective: Leicester’s was a points tally surpassed only by Reading (106 pts.) in the 2004/05 Championship season and matched by Newcastle United in the 2008/09 Championship season. Of course, points collected in the Championship prior to promotion are irrelevant and never significant predictors of a newly-promoted team’s future performances in the grueling Premier League. Still, my excitement did not abate, and, just like the visceral image of Emile Heskey kicking the ball out of the stadium, I still don’t know why.

What I do know is that Leicester are ambitious. The acquisition of Inter Milan stalwart Esteban Cambiasso last season was a sign of this ambition. Cambiasso is no longer at Leicester this season, but the philosophy behind his transfer remains. After their fairytale “rise-from-the-dead” drama last season, the club has continued to construct their team with some eye-raising acquisitions. And so far, it is paying off.

The Foxes still remain undefeated after five EPL games and, to cap it all off, they are now in second behind Manchester City in the EPL table, who are undefeated themselves with a perfect win record. Leicester have also equalled City’s goal tally of 11, although they have conceded seven compared to City’s five consecutive clean sheets. While their defense need some additional time on the practice pitches, their attack has been rather impressive. Riyad Mahrez, in particular, has been sensational from the get-go along with Jamie Vardy, who spearheads the Foxes’ attack. As a unit, though, despite their defensive struggles, they are bound to improve even further as the young EPL season steamrolls onwards. Additionally, when Gökhan Inler and N’Golo Kanté both settle down in this Leicester team, it is highly expected that their defense will also undergo an improvement.

It is a scary thought for opposing managers to devise an effective game plan against Leicester, given the Foxes potential to amalgamate offensive intent and defensive caretaking. On paper, the Leicester squad looks very solid, if not spectacular. Some smart acquisitions, covering all areas of the squad, will go a long way in ensuring their competitiveness in the Premier League. Couple that with a proven tactician in manager Claudio Ranieri and we could be looking at another club trying to emulate what Swansea City and Southampton have done since their promotion.

Sure, Ranieri might not have convinced his (many) critics this season, but I would implore those cynics to refer to his stellar record during his time at the French side Monaco. I would like to think that the Leicester faithful are going to see the Monacan version of their manager, and that can only be a positive for the club. When the end of the season arrives, the EPL table, as it stands after matchday 5, will not be the same (although we can deduce that the top three teams will be Man City, Arsenal, and Manchester United). Leicester will not finish in the top four, no matter how much you believe in fairy tales.

A Europa League place could be achievable, but I wouldn’t get my hopes up too much. The more realistic achievement, however, is for Leicester to finish with a greater points tally than that of last season. If the Foxes accomplish the latter, then they have a great chance of finishing in mid-table. Of course, a top ten finish would be much more favorable.

That kind of success will be a just reward for Leicester’s ambition, not to mention the financial windfall that’ll occur with a higher EPL finish. It will also make the project they are creating at the moment more attractive for talents, whether they are local or abroad. In a league that’s overrun by non-stop media coverage for the “top six” clubs - Liverpool, Man United, Chelsea (despite their recent dreadful form), Man City, Arsenal, and Tottenham - Leicester, who are clearly a few galaxies away from “top six” classification, finishing mid-table (or better) will be a welcome storyline both in King Power Stadium and England.
