Toffees Primed for Merseyside Derby

The dramatic come-from-behind 3-2 victory at West Brom signifies Everton’s galvanized form

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

Did you know that Everton F.C. have had a few nicknames to their credit? Casual fans will identify Everton as “the Toffees,” which is the nickname most synonymous with the club. But what about “the Blues”? That particular sobriquet seems to be ascribed to Chelsea already, but, little did I know, Everton has just as much claim to it! Try “The School of Science” or “The People’s Club” and real people will be left scratching their heads. Who knew Everton had a perennially cool nickname like “The School of Science”? I certainly didn’t.

Glorious nicknames aside, Everton were simply awe-inspiring against West Brom at the Hawthorns. A come-from-behind win while playing away from home is, in my eyes, always impressive. Thanks to that victory, the Toffees are now up to fifth in the Premier League table, higher than their inner-city arch-rivals Liverpool, who are ninth, albeit with only a one point difference. Both clubs are slated to clash this upcoming weekend in the first Merseyside derby of the 2015-16 Premier League season.

The derby couldn’t have come at a better time for Everton and its fans. They are undefeated in six straight games now in all competitions, which includes that resounding 3-1 drubbing of Chelsea at Goodison Park. The fact that they achieved that amazing result against Chelsea at home will be cause for concern at Liverpool. Liverpool are also riding high from their own 3-2 result against Aston Villa, which insured their manager Brendan Rodgers’ job for one more week. But they need to build on that momentum and not simply rest on their laurels.

Believe it or not, Roberto Martinez hasn’t registered a win against Liverpool in the Premier League since he took over Everton back in 2013. His record against them stands at three draws and one loss, with that one loss being a 4-0 thrashing back in January 2014. Liverpool’s man-of-the-hour, Daniel Sturridge, scored a brace back then, flanked by Steven Gerrard and Luis Suarez goals (one apiece). However, both Stevie G. and Luis S. have left Anfield and, in that process, have been inadequately replaced by the club.

Martinez, therefore, will be salivating at the prospect of recording his first ever Premier League victory over Liverpool in his Everton career. Given that the Reds are currently at their most vulnerable and that the Toffees have been rock solid this season, the moment to strike a win over their hated rivals will be on every Evertonian mind. Perhaps, Everton are still atoning for their dismal performance of the season prior. Not that they didn’t play well or anything. The distraction of Europe may well have stretched Everton, hence their 11th-place finish in the Premier League last season. However, there’s no European action for them this season, so their focus will be fully on the league.

Unlike Liverpool, Everton can rely on a steady head in Roberto Martinez. While Brendan Rodgers is under severe pressure at Liverpool, Roberto Martinez has been a calming presence for Everton. He has been tactically assured since the get go, carrying out some shrewd business in the transfer market and, when there has been pressure, he has handled it fairly well. The prime example of this is Martinez’s handling of the “John Stones to Chelsea” transfer saga this past summer. In the end, Martinez and Everton ended up holding on to their star young defender while simultaneously and brilliantly spurning José Mourinho’s Chelsea.

As the derby day gets closer and closer, the questions faced by Martinez are more pleasant than those faced by Rodgers. The only worries for the Everton manager are concerns regarding the fitness of John Stones and Seamus Coleman, and who should start on either side of the young talents Ross Barkley and Romelu Lukaku in attack. Both of these worries are warranted, but adequately covered.

Should John Stones and Seamus Coleman be deemed unfit to make the squad - something highly unlikely, I might add - they have reliable replacements in Ramiro Funes Mori and either Tony Hibbert or Tyias Browning. As for the questions regarding who should flank Barkley and Lukaku in attack, they have arisen more due to the wealth of talent available for Martinez’s selection, and not for the lack of it. In any case, it seems as if Gerard Deulofeu and Steven Naismith will be starting, with adequate backups in Aaron Lennon, Arouna Kone, Aiden McGeady and Kevin Mirallas.

Of course, this shouldn’t detract from the potential threat that Liverpool pose. They are by no means a pushover team; it’s just that they are a bit unstable at the moment. The return of Sturridge is great timing for Rodgers and Liverpool, and it buys some much-needed time for the over-pressured Rodgers to figure things out. However, a Martinez-led Everton will be looking to capitalize on this golden opportunity and, in the process, put a severe dent on Liverpool’s aspirations to return to the Champions League.

If Martinez pulls this off, then that victory will be sweeter than any toffee in Liverpool.
