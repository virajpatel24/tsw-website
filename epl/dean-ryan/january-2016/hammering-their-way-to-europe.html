Hammering Their Way to Europe

West Ham extended their unbeaten streak to eight games with their 3-1 victory over Bournemouth

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

“What kind of ham did you say?”

— United States “Vice President” Selina Meyer, during a conversation with a local British bartender on her working visit to London

The above excerpt was, of course, taken from the critically acclaimed HBO comedy series Veep. The hilarity that ensues in that bar scene was very memorable, but West Ham fans shouldn’t feel aggrieved. Sure, it was convenient for the writers to make a joke out of the word ham, but at least they got publicity. Bad publicity you say? Nonsense. You should know: There’s no such thing as bad publicity. With their club looking to qualify for Europe and also planning to move into the state-of-the-art Olympic Stadium next season, they’ll need all the publicity they can get.

With their come-from-behind 3-1 victory over Bournemouth on Matchday 21, West Ham catapulted into fifth place with 39 points, leapfrogging Manchester United in the process, who got stuck in a 3-3 tie with Newcastle. This victory by the Hammers becomes their third in a row in the largely unpredictable Premier League. It becomes their fourth if you include the 1-0 FA Cup victory over Wolves last Saturday. They also broke a club record, with their unbeaten streak stretching to eight games, a run that started back in November. This remarkable achievement has no doubt put them on solid ground to challenge for a European spot, whether it be the Champions League or the Europa League. In fact, their lofty aspirations are actually much more realistic and serious than those of the highly ambitious, but inconsistent Stoke City.

What makes the Hammers’ unbeaten run impressive is that they did it without their talisman, Dimitri Payet. Ever since they lost Payet to injury on November 7th, West Ham have only lost once (a 4-1 drubbing by Spurs in the next match). After that, though, they regrouped and embarked on their current unbeaten streak, implying that the loss was only stemming from a bedding-in period; once they adjusted their tactics to cope with Payet’s absence, the positive performances ensued from Slaven Bilić’s side. Injuries to other key players like Winston Reid, Manuel Lanzini, and Diafra Sakho threatened to derail West Ham’s campaign, too. This club-wide upheaval resulted in five straight draws at the beginning of the streak, with the team gaining the unwanted moniker of “a draw specialist.”

While it may look deplorable initially, their manager Slaven Bilić has tightened the Hammers’ defense in light of the depletions of their attacking options. Over the course of their current eight game unbeaten streak, they’ve only conceded four goals, with impressive clean sheets recorded against Manchester United, Stoke, and Liverpool. With Winston Reid absent, the defense has been led superbly by James Collins, being partnered by former Juventus defender Angelo Ogbonna. James Tomkins being pushed to the right back position has also done wonders for their defense, making it more sturdy in the process. Add to that the screen of Mark Noble and Cheikhou Kouyaté in midfield and West Ham’s goal became almost impenetrable.

With the defence sorted out, Bilić’s only worry was to make something happen in attack. However, Michail Antonio, Enner Valencia, and even Andy Carroll stepped up to the plate to fill in the void. This resulted in the club finding their way to the win column again during the congested Christmas period, with their 2-0 victory over Liverpool - on January 2nd of this year - being the most impressive. The return of Dimitri Payet in that match was also a welcoming sight for fans, with their star player making a cameo appearance to gauge his fitness level. This led to Slaven Bilić proclaiming that he doesn’t need to dip into the transfer market in January, simultaneously prioritizing the permanent signing of Manuel Lanzini and placing his faith in the current crop of players at his disposal.

At the Vitality Stadium, home of Bournemouth, Dimitri Payet’s first start since his injury back in November was magnificent at best. He assisted on the Enner Valencia goal and scored a great free kick, taking his tally to six goals and four assists for the season. Valencia himself added another one later on with a fabulous free kick of his own, continuing his red hot form since the dawn of the new year. While Andy Carroll was lost to injury in the early stages of the game, his replacement, Nikica Jelavić more than adequately led the line for the Hammers attack. Not lacking any inspiration after having scored his first goal for the club in the FA Cup, Jelavić is the right player at the right time for West Ham, if Carroll’s injury is worse than expected.

So there you have it, West Ham are knocking at the door to Europe, with a hammer no less. Made up of a supportive and ambitious boardroom, and a charismatic and determined young manager in Slaven Bilić, odds are they will break through the door sooner rather than later. With the imminent move to the Olympic Stadium (initial capacity at 54,000) next season, the stage is set for the club to go on a European adventure. Not to mention the money that comes along with it, a fact that was stated by the club’s vice-chairman, Karren Brady. Maybe with all that money, they can put more effort into marketing the club overseas, like in the United States, where there seems to be some sort of a fan base already. If nothing else, then the London-based club already has a life-long fan in the WWE’s executive vice president Triple H.

“West Ham is probably the closest to what we do - the passion of their fanbase and their loyalty. That’s the closest to what we do, at WWE we have the most passionate, loyal fans of all time, but that might be debatable with West Ham.”

— Triple H, expounding to ITV on his love for the London-based club
