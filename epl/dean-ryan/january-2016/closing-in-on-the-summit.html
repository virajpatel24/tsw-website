Closing in on the Summit

Three second-half goals sealed the deal for Spurs as they clinched a 3-1 come-from-behind win over Crystal Palace

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

For a moment there at Selhurst Park, the scoreline was destined to be a disappointing 1-1 draw. While Palace would have relished the lone point, Spurs would’ve been left ruing a missed opportunity. In the crazy climate that is the 2015-16 English Premier League season, every point earned counts a little bit more towards a team’s cause. In the case of Tottenham, their cause is to push for the number one spot. If they eventually fail to reach that lofty goal, then a return to the Champions League would be a plausible target. If they fail to do that, too, then their season will probably be deemed a complete failure.

After finishing fifth last season, it is imperative that Spurs build on that by qualifying for Europe’s most elite competition this season. With Chelsea currently off battling out of a mid-table finish, with Manchester United not performing to their standards, and with Liverpool currently in rebuilding mode for the umpteenth time, the timing will never be better for Tottenham to capitalize. That is why a 1-1 draw against Crystal Palace would have been a disappointing result for them. Then, came the moment of magic. Dele Alli, the young stalwart of the Lilywhites, scored a wonderful, mid-range goal in the 84th minute, a goal so great that it shocked the scorer himself to the point that he didn’t even know how to celebrate.

“I didn’t really think about it too much and I sort of shocked myself - I think you could tell from my celebration, I didn’t really know what to do.”

— Dele Alli, telling SpursTV about his paradigm-shifting goal

Much to the chagrin of Crystal Palace, it didn’t end with Alli’s goal. Nacer Chadli added gloss to Tottenham’s victory with a late, late goal in the 94th minute to fully seal the emphatic comeback victory. It is easy to overlook the Belgian’s stellar contribution in the match, given that the majority of plaudits were directed towards Alli. It is Chadli’s entry into the match in the 56th minute that changed the complexion of the game; Mauricio Pochettino sent out Chadli in place of Eric Dier in what proved to be a tactical masterstroke by the head man in their bid for victory. With Chadli on the field, Alli was afforded more space to operate and his subsequent assist on Harry Kane’s goal ensured that Spurs drew level just three minutes after the hour mark.

Tottenham are now in fourth, five points clear of Manchester United (5th place). At the same time, they have reeled in the struggling Manchester City and their North London rivals, Arsenal, to within two points. The top-of-the-table Leicester Foxes are only five points away, too. “Comfortable” would be the best word to describe Spurs’ position in the league right now, seeing that they are secure from whatever challenges that may come from teams below them while simultaneously being within striking distance of the number one spot.

They do possess one of the best records in the league at the moment, with only three losses incurred, second only to Leicester, who have accumulated two. Their inability to convert draws into wins seems to have been alleviated as of late, with back-to-back wins over Sunderland and now Crystal Palace ensuring that their win column keeps ticking positively. Their record now stands at eleven wins and nine draws. A draw at Selhurst Park would have seen them back on level pegging in their wins and draws column, but thankfully, that didn’t happen thanks to Alli’s magic.

With less than a week left in the January transfer window, one has to wonder whether Spurs needed any more strengthening in order to really consolidate their position in the top four. A backup for Harry Kane is sorely needed, with the player being ever-present in every league match. Sooner or later, he will require a much-needed rest. Otherwise, Spurs risk overplaying a player who may eventually succumb to injury, like the way Alexis Sánchez did for Arsenal these past few months. As the January Transfer Window draws to a close, names like Jackson Martínez of Atletico Madrid has been thrown around as of late, although whether it comes to fruition or not remains to be seen.

“For me when you spend more than £15m, £16m, £18m you must have a player that brings good things: good behaviour, discipline, responsibility, professionalism. It's important to get it right. If we spend £25m or £30m we need to be sure this player can have the kind of impact that Gareth Bale or Cristiano Ronaldo has on Real Madrid. Or Messi or Neymar for Barcelona. He'd have to be our best player. It's impossible to sign players for this amount and wait for his performance.”

— Spurs manager Mauricio Pochettino, discussing his potential transfer activity

Whoever it is that they may or may not ultimately sign, Tottenham fans can rest easy with Mauricio Pochettino at the helm. He has been outstanding for them this season, especially in his handling of the young players, integrating them into the squad and extracting the most out of their performances. The fact that the North London side is challenging for the title - with a relatively young team - while still presenting a challenge in the FA Cup and Europa League also speaks volumes about Pochettino’s tactical acumen. Oh, and the possibility that he is secretly reading this piece and listening to the podcasts has made Señor Pochettino that much more compelling, in my eyes. Here’s to a great season, Spurs.
