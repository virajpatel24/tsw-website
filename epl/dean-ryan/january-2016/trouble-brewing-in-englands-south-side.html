Trouble Brewing in England’s South Side

The 1-0 loss to Norwich exposed some underlying problems at Southampton

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

The first game of the new year saw Southampton suffer yet another loss in their stuttering Premier League campaign. While there’s still some ways to go for the Saints before they can match last season’s loss tally (eight compared to the 14 from last season), it is the manner in which they are inching closer to that number (and beyond) that is cause for alarm. The Saints’ form in the past eight games now stands at one win, one draw, and six losses, with the lone win coming in the form of an unexpected 4-0 thrashing of league leaders Arsenal. That victory on Boxing Day was supposed to serve as the turning point in their season. But Ronald Koeman’s players resumed their losing ways once again in the subsequent meetings against West Ham on December 28, 2015 and against Norwich this previous Saturday.

It is difficult to pinpoint what exactly is the cause of Southampton’s rut, especially in the aftermath of the Arsenal game as 2015 came to a close. At the turn of the year, however, things have begun to unravel a bit. A combination of indiscipline, lack of focus, injuries to key players, and a general lack of ambition have been revealed, thanks to the comments from the gaffer himself in the past few days. With the opening of the January transfer window, the time is nigh for corrective measures to be taken in what could be a crucial month that ultimately makes or breaks their season, lest they want to get stuck in a relegation battle with the likes of Chelsea.

“I have been let down by some key players. My last message to [Mané] was that he needed to be concentrated and focused. You have a responsibility as a player not just to me as the manager, but your teammates, fans and the technical staff to show respect. I am angry about players who are not focused and concentrated and that is even more disappointing than the result today. It is not the first time that this has happened.”

— Ronald Koeman, ranting on Sadio Mané after the 1-0 loss to Norwich

Sadio Mané was, of course, dropped to the bench after appearing late for a team meeting prior to the match with Norwich. This prompted Koeman to alter many things tactically and, therefore, these last-minute alterations contributed to the eventual loss against the Canaries. While certain quarters deem this outburst by Mr. Koeman as an overreaction to a player’s failure in keeping time, if one were to delve deeper into the comments made by the gaffer, it is more than just a timekeeping issue. “Distracted” would be the perfect word to describe the Saints’ star player.

Exceptional performances over one and a half seasons at St. Mary’s Stadium will surely attract a lot of suitors. Southampton are, after all, a selling club, with its players deemed to be of astute quality. It seemed natural that a player like Mané would want to make a move to a bigger club when the right offer came. But forcing things through is not the right way, just ask Saido Berahino over at West Brom. While there have been better stories, like Raheem Sterling and Manchester City, there is no guarantee that the Saints’ speed merchant will end up like that, with the probability of being left to rot on the bench of a top-tier club still there for the taking.

Another player on the receiving end of the wrath of Koeman is defensive midfielder Victor Wanyama. His bloody-mindedness in getting sent off at Carrow Road paved the way for a Norwich victory. While the player himself has apologized via instagram, it remains to be seen whether this will result in a much more improved performance further down the road. Wanyama’s performances this season have not been up to par when compared to his shifts from last season, with only his aggressiveness remaining consistent. His presence is more like a man frustrated ever since he was denied a summer transfer to Spurs. This kind of petulance is definitely holding back the Saints’ cause, with his actions probably aiming for the same end result as that of Mané’s: To transfer to an even bigger club.

Another factor that has increased Southampton’s current woes is the injury to their star striker Graziano Pellè. That Pellè injury - and the lack of a competent striker to cover this glaring hole in the Saints’ attack - has contributed to the lack of a cutting edge in the final third of the pitch. The brace against Arsenal aside, Shane Long is simply not the right man to lead the attack. Spanish summer recruit Juanmi hasn’t been up to par to the rigors of the English premier League while Jay Rodriguez spends more time in the doctor’s office than on the field itself. This was noted by Mr. Koeman himself when he was asked whether the club will delve into the January transfer market in search of a striker:

“Like I mentioned before, if we need to sign a player, it needs to be a striker. But that also depends on the situation of Jay and what will happen with Graziano. It’s too early to make conclusions about that. We still have to wait to see how they recover and how long it takes, and then we’ll make a decision about what we have to do.”

— Ronald Koeman, on the Saints’ potential transfer activity in January

While the answer to the question of who the club will target remains a mystery, it is imperative for Mr. Koeman to address other issues as well, such as the potential departure of Sadio Manè and Victor Wanyama. Sorting out their futures, sooner rather than later, should be the top priority in order to maintain the squad harmony and morale before the demoralized duo inflict more damage to the rest of the squad. This will also enable the club to sign their suitable replacements sooner as well. With Southampton in 13th place on 24 points, they’re still in a solid position to turn their season around. Well then, chop chop, Mr. Koeman.
