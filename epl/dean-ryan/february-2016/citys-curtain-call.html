City’s Curtain Call

Manchester City’s 3-1 defeat to Leicester at the Etihad indicates a major struggle for motivation

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

Ever since Manchester City made the announcement that Pep Guardiola will be taking over the reins at the Etihad starting this summer, you can’t help but feel the heightened sense of anxiety among the players and their current manager, Manuel Pellegrini. For the Chilean manager, it’s one thing to live and breathe in the corridors of uncertainty, but now that the equation has been taken away from him, one has to wonder: What is going on in that grizzled mind of his? Knowing the City manager, it is hard to interpret what his true feelings just might be - with the media unable to coax themselves to pry open his sacred thoughts on serious questions about his unknown future - because he is a very charming man who remains mum on most issues.

For the players, some are already planning for newer, greener pastures already, with Yaya Touré being the most notable example. Meanwhile, the others are preparing to fight for their future roster places once the highly anticipated Guardiola era begins. Players like Joe Hart, Sergio Agüero, and David Silva can rest easy, though, given that these three will be the probable cogs of a well-oiled English tiki-taka engine. Whatever the case may be, a massive upheaval is on the cards, meaning that the run in till the end of the season will be a curtain call of sorts. All of these questions being posed of the Manchester club and its future leaders are threatening to make-or-break the remainder of their season. With City still competing for a quadruple, how will they respond from here on out? More importantly, what will provide their motivation?

If the recent loss against Claudio Ranieri’s Leicester on Saturday is anything to go by, then Sergio Agüero will be that source. The only problem, though, is that not everyone on that City squad is as motivated as Kun Agüero. Cue the aforementioned Yaya Touré, who has been a far cry from the domineering force of a midfielder he once was. How City wished for N’Golo Kanté to be the one on their side at the Etihad. Masterful defensively and a driving force moving forward, Kanté is the kind of complete midfielder that the 32-year-old Touré once was for City. Instead, as the Leicester match indicated, the Citizens are left with a player that’s a shell of his former self, a fact that’s backed by Pellegrini having to pull him out (along with the sedate Fabian Delph) in the 51st minute.

The defense was in shambles, too. The Argentine defensive pairing of Martín Demichelis and Nicolás Otamendi was rendered inadequate in the face of Leicester’s repeated attacks. And both were responsible for all three of the Foxes’s goals. Their opposite numbers, the unheralded pair of Robert Huth and Wes Morgan, meanwhile produced a masterclass in defending while facing the dangerous trio of Agüero, David Silva, and Raheem Sterling, essentially restricting City’s three-pronged monster to only one goal, which came in the dying minutes of the match. These underwhelming performances, across the board, from City’s world-class talent will have certainly alerted the incoming Pep Guardiola, who is already probably taking copious notes of everyone on the squad.

It can be assumed that all of the uncertainty regarding who will stay and who will depart is destabilizing the current City squad, in terms of morale. Maybe some of them will question their own abilities to fit in when the Pep era rolls around. After all, we are talking about a manager in Guardiola whose innovative approach to football requires a bit of versatility from all involved on the field. Players like Javier Mascherano, Lionel Messi, David Alaba, and Javi Martínez are proof of players that not only fit the Guardiola mould, but also examples of players sacrificing their previous roles for newer ones: Mascherano moved from CDM to center-back, Messi from right winger to central striker, Alaba from left-back to defensive midfielder (at times), and Martínez from CDM to center-back. The caliber of raw talent in just those four superstars I listed should make players with limited abilities on the current City squad - like Martín Demichelis, Wilfried Bony, and Fernando - that much more uncomfortable, given their recent underwhelming performances for City.

It shouldn’t have to be this way. Despite the humbling defeat to Leicester this past weekend, Man City are still on course for four different titles. Plus, they have Manuel Pellegrini at the helm. That man deserves a decent send off, at the very least. All the players should realize that they can (and should) focus on this season and fight for their current manager till the end. Yaya Touré, in particular, should take note of this, considering all of the faith that was placed on him by Pellegrini through thick and thin. Besides, seeing out the current City title hunt to the best of their capabilities can only leave them in good standing, despite whether they stay at the Etihad or leave. In fact, they don’t have to search too far for a blueprint on how to sketch out their next three months of football: They can simply follow in the footsteps of the 2012-13 Bayern Munich squad that won the treble for their outgoing manager, Jupp Heynckes, before Pep arrived in Munich that summer from Barcelona.

By all means, the season is not over yet by any stretch of the imagination. The title is not yet safe in the bag for the flying Foxes, and the Citizens should already know that by now. Certainly, after being under Manuel Pellegrini for almost three seasons now, they would have been familiar on what they are capable of producing, especially when Vincent Kompany and Kevin de Bruyne comes back from their respective injuries. The gaffer would do well to remind them of their capabilities, not least when faced with such insurmountable odds. Their now traditional late season surge is yet to come - which usually starts around April - when they have already proven they can win the title outright (2013-14 season at the expense of Liverpool) or at least reach second place (2014-15 season behind Chelsea).

With plenty of tough challenges ahead in the month of February, and this being their final swansong together under Pellegrini, the time is nigh for the Citizens to kickstart their curtain call tour. That tour will commence this upcoming Sunday evening against fellow title challengers Tottenham Hotspur. In this match, they could set the tone for what’s to come down the road - matches against Chelsea in the FA Cup, Dynamo Kiev in the UEFA Champions League, and Liverpool in the Capital One Cup final. And if they do achieve that prestigious quadruple along the way? Well, that would provide a great, well-deserved ending for that charming Citizen named Manuel Pellegrini.
