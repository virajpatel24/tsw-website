Just the One Striker

City lacked the cutting edge as they drew 0-0 against Aston Villa at Villa Park

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

It was supposed to be a hard-fought victory for the visitors after their exertions against Sevilla in the UEFA Champions League in midweek. Hard-fought because of the Rémi Garde factor on the Villans’ bench. The new Villa manager brings with him innovative ideas, but with only three training sessions spent with the players before the Sunday match against City, a touch of uncertainty still permeates throughout the club. An uncertainty which is good for Villa, but bad for Manchester City. The Citizens had no idea what kind of Aston Villa are they were facing, be it tactics-wise or personnel-wise.

“There are no easy games in the Premier League, especially against Aston Villa. Always when you have a new manager, you have some reaction. They’re at the bottom, they have a new manager, so they have the motivation not to get relegated.”

— Manuel Pellegrini, while addressing the media on Friday

Making only one change to the lineup that started against Sevilla in the Champions League match midweek - Kevin de Bruyne coming in for Jesús Navas - Pellegrini may have been expecting a victory, however hard-fought it could be. Then, fate decided to intervene and their lone striker up front, Wilfried Bony, pulled up injured while attempting to chase a through ball in the 25th minute. With that injury, City were suddenly without an out-and-out striker and thus, it completely disrupted their original game plan. Jesús Navas came on for the injured Bony, entering the match earlier than expected while Raheem Sterling was pushed into a makeshift center forward role.

While Sterling is a capable goalscorer (he has four EPL goals this season, which are two more than both Chelsea’s Diego Costa (2 goals) and Manchester United’s Wayne Rooney (2 goals, too)), it has to be noted that he scored his goals by cutting in from his favorite left wing position. This was evident when he decided to cut inside instead of shooting when put through on goal by Yaya Touré, even though he was already in a central position. He was clipped by Villa defender Ciaran Clark instead, opting to make a weak penalty claim off the incident. With the events unfolding near the half-time mark, it would have been a huge relief to score a goal. While he did well to ghost into the penalty box to head in Navas’s cross early in the second half, it was savagely saved by Brad Guzan’s face (OUCHHH!!). Fernando’s shot off the crossbar in injury time summed up Manchester City’s woes in front of goal as they ended up drawing the match 0-0.

While the Citizens still remained on top of the table (thanks to Arsenal drawing 1-1 against Spurs), the inherent problem in the striking department can’t be overlooked. Believe it or not, a club of City’s stature is currently short on one quality striker. Just one. Take away the injured Sergio Agüero and now Bony, they literally have nobody to lead the attack. How this situation is allowed to play out and is not already addressed via the summer transfer window is inconceivable. It’s not like they are short of money.

That said, what is done is done. City can only look to the January transfer window to bolster their insufficient striking department. Agüero is of course the premier striker for City, so they will simply need to buy a quality backup to compete with Bony. Not to mention someone who is not cup-tied to play in the UEFA Champions League. All this makes it a tricky proposition to fulfill, with the dearth of quality striking talent available in January. The same question was posed to me by beIN Sports:

Why not, right? Robin van Persie has ample experience playing in the Premier League, Champions League, you name it. He definitely possesses quality, while at the same time he wouldn’t infringe upon Sergio Agüero’s status as the top dog at the Etihad. But, much more importantly, he would be immediately available to play in the UEFA Champions League as well, which is a huge target for City this season. Plus, by signing him, City can put one over their cross-town rivals Manchester United (who transferred the 32-year-old Dutch striker to the Turkish club Fenerbaçhe earlier in the summer), spiting the Red Devils and Louis van Gaal in the process!

Van Persie isn’t likely to come cheap however, with his weekly wages reportedly in the €250,000 stratosphere. As I said before in this column, it is very hard to find quality strikers in January, let alone ones that are also qualified for Champions League play. But perhaps, that is what City needs to pay for in order to win the Premier League and, on a much more ambitious level, the Champions League.
