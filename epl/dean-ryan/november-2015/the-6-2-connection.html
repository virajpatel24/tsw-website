The 6-2 Connection

Arouna Koné scores a hat-trick as The Toffees thrash Sunderland

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

It was nearly three weeks ago that we saw the same scoreline. The venue was St. James’ Park at the time. The match was between Newcastle United and Norwich, with the former prevailing 6-2. This time, the venue was Goodison Park and the match was played between Everton and Sunderland. Same scoreline, different venues, different teams, different circumstances. So, what connects both of these matches?

Apart from the scoreline, I can’t really think of anything connecting them. Except, maybe Sunderland. Yes, Sunderland. Notice the flow from Newcastle’s 6-2 victory, to the 3-0 loss to Sunderland and now, Sam Allardyce’s men lost to Everton 6-2. Conspiracy theorists and, maybe to a certain extent, gamblers will allude to this flow and say that it is all connected somehow. Perhaps they’ll say that after this, Everton will lose 3-0 to their next opponent (West Ham), if the pattern is to continue. Such simple-mindedness or in the conspiracy theorist case, over-elaborateness, is very sad to bear witness to while at the same time being very amusing.

The Toffees’ huge victory is by no means part of an ongoing imaginary trend or one that is perpetrated by the invisible hands of the infamous Illuminati. No. It is down to an excellent team effort, tireless work ethic and, of course, clinical finishing from the sensational Arouna Koné. He may not have finished with four goals like Georginio Wijnaldum did in Newcastle’s 6-2 win, but he was excellent nonetheless, finishing the night with three goals, one assist and an eighty nine percent pass success rate to boot.

Equally impressive on the night, if not as phenomenal, was Romelu Lukaku. The striker managed to score a goal and two assists on the night as he looked to repay his manager’s faith in him in light of recent comments made by his agent, Mino Raiola, regarding his future status. In spite of the distraction, Lukaku should do well to focus more on his game considering that Euro 2016 is taking place next summer. With Christian Benteke returning for Liverpool and Michy Batshuayi emerging at Marseille in French Ligue 1, every appearance and performance will be under scrutiny from Belgium coach Marc Wilmots in order to determine who will spearhead the attack come the Euros. And with showings like this every game, Lukaku will certainly be the go to choice to lead the Belgian attack.

Speaking of repaying the gaffer’s faith, Gerard Deulofeu seems to be getting the hang of it as well. On this particular night, everything clicked for him and he ended the game with a goal and an assist, highlighting his impact on the team when he is on song. Now, all he needs to do is to retain this level of performance in every game, something that has eluded him thus far in his career. He’ll do well to learn about consistency from James McCarthy, who was his usual imperious self at the heart of Everton’s midfield.

“I am here to adapt and learn, and so far I am enjoying it a lot. To be honest I didn’t think I would break into the first-team so quickly but as a footballer you have to be ready for these moments.”

— Ramiro Funes Mori, on slotting into Everton’s defense

If any Toffees fans were worried when they received the news that captain Phil Jagielka would be out for two months, then they were in for a pleasant surprise. The Argentinian Ramiro Funes Mori has slotted in seamlessly into the heart of the back four and looks set to be a mainstay of the defense - at least until Jagielka’s return. With the future of John Stones up in the air after Chelsea’s heavy flirting this past transfer window, the capture of Funes Mori from River Plate seems like a masterstroke for Roberto Martínez. Instead of just two reliable centre backs, he now has three to choose from between Stones, Jagielka, and Funes Mori. This will present a dilemma of sorts for the manager further down the road, but, for now, Funes Mori is revelling in the spotlight.

Everton currently sit ninth on the Premier League table, just one point and place below Jürgen Klopp’s Liverpool. They will face West Ham next weekend at the Boleyn Ground. The Hammers lost this past weekend to Watford and will look to rebound strongly. With the circumstances surrounding the West Ham vs. Everton fixture, it will add fuel to the fire that Everton will indeed lose 3-0 to West Ham. While I think this is highly unlikely, it is still quite possible because hey, this is football. And football is patently unpredictable.
