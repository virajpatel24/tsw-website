Citizens Crushed by the Red Tide

Liverpool humiliates City 4-1 at the Etihad

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

Last week, I was asked in the Sports Whorl podcast, how do I rate Jürgen Klopp’s first few games in charge of Liverpool? I answered “Out of ten, a solid seven.” If I was asked the same question after Liverpool’s demolition of Manchester City this past weekend? I’d definitely upgrade my answer to a solid eight out of ten. I’m still hesitant to award a nine or a perfect ten just yet, but they’ll get there soon enough, given they produce more of these kind of remarkable performances.

The Reds are on an upward trajectory at the moment, fueled by the larger-than-life personality of the one and only Jürgen Klopp. Even after that stunning defeat against Crystal Palace just before the international break, there still exists a sense of positive energy amongst the players. Although the fans abandoned them in the last few minutes of that match at Anfield, Klopp, despite feeling pretty alone at the time, didn’t let that unsympathetic act from his own fans deter him one bit.

“After 82 minutes, came the goal. With 12 minutes to go I saw many people leaving the stadium. I felt pretty alone at this moment. We decide when it is over. Between 82 and 94 minutes, you can make 8 goals, if you like. I am not disappointed about this, and they have reasons. Maybe it is easier to get away before the end, I don’t know. But we should be responsible that nobody can leave the stadium a minute before because everything can happen. We have to show that and we didn’t.”

— Klopp, after Liverpool's 2-1 defeat against Crystal Palace at Anfield

Even in sadness the man still exudes positivity. However, at the time I simply took these words as a valiant attempt to divert attention away from Liverpool’s performance in that defeat. It is because of this defeat that made me proclaim so resoundingly that Klopp and his charges wouldn’t stand a chance against City after the international break. I thought they simply were not ready yet. Oh how I was about to be proven wrong. Forced to choose between the City vs Liverpool match and the ‘El Clasico’ being played in Spain, I decided to leave it to fate. Sure enough, the ‘ol watering hole - of which I am a frequent visitor of - chose the former.

I swear to God that Herr Klopp is facing the camera just to send a message to me. Heck, if he were to shout “Are you not entertained?!?,” I would’ve been compelled to answer back with a resounding “YES!!!” Of course, this is just my imagination running wild. However, the match itself more than made up for missing ‘El Clasico.’ Of course Liverpool and City are nowhere near the caliber of Barcelona and Real Madrid, but the entertainment value was just as satisfying, if not even more so for the Reds fans.

The partnership of Philippe Coutinho and Roberto Firmino bodes well for the near future, if their performance at the Etihad is any indication. Together, they plundered the City backline and got themselves a goal and an assist each. What’s more impressive is the way they seem to be on the same wavelengths throughout when attacking, as evidenced by them assisting each other’s goals. This could be the sort of partnership that Liverpool has been looking for ever since the dismantling of the mighty ‘SAS’ duo of Luis Suarez and Daniel Sturridge.

However, the Brazilian connection of Coutinho and Firmino wasn’t the only bright point in Liverpool’s excellent attack. Adam Lallana was great throughout, too, exploiting the acres of space left behind by Aleksandar Kolarov and linking well with his Merseyside teammates in attack. Come to think of it, the trio of Lallana, Firmino, and Coutinho could call themselves the ‘LFC’ trio, provided that they play together for a while. Let’s hope no one from this fragile new trio changes clubs, gets injured, or gets themselves severely out of form. Because LFC is the one acronym that definitely needs to come alive in the near future.

Lallana, of course, is just one of the players improving by leaps and bounds since the arrival of Klopp to Kop. Emre Can has been restored to his more natural central midfield spot after the three-man defense was discarded, and he has slowly grown back into this role. This culminated in a highly balanced performance against City, with his backheel pass to Coutinho that led to the Firmino goal that served as proverbial the icing on the cake. I should also commend the rest of the gegenpressing Liverpool XI and the subs as well for such a vast improvement in such a short time. That this complete performance came against the likes of Manchester City is quite an achievement already.

There’s no telling how much improvement the current Liverpool squad can undergo after such a convincing all-around victory. I am salivating at the prospect of how much more potent the Liverpool attack can get once Daniel Sturridge and, though it may be a long time to go, Danny Ings return. Not to mention the variety of tactics Klopp can deploy with such an array of attacking talent consisting of Coutinho, Firmino, Lallana, Benteke and Ibe. The midfield and defense could do with a little work, although so far, things are going well at the moment. The whole squad is already improving and I shudder at the thought that we haven’t even reached the January transfer window yet. And we can be sure that Klopp will take full advantage of Liverpool’s cash reserves when that time arrives.

You may think that I have gone bonkers for salivating over the aforementioned talent. This may be the “Klopp effect” rubbing off on me, enveloping me in an aura of unbridled positivity, but the results on the field speaks for themselves (unlike, say, a Jose Mourinho, who does speak for his results, players, and everything else in between). You can see the difference in the work rate already in the approach and the hunger shown by the players themselves, who have clearly picking up what Klopp’s putting down. The Reds currently sit ninth in the EPL table, with 20 points, and they will surely hope to add more further down the road. With Swansea visiting them at Anfield this weekend, it’s high time for the Kop to embrace the Klopp era, and hopefully, stay in their seats till the very end. Who knows, they may be in for a huge scoreline. Just make sure to sit till the final whistle.
