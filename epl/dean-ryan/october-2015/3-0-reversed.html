3-0 Reversed

Ander Herrera stars in United’s triumph over Everton at Goodison Park

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

“I am very happy with the response of my players and I am very proud of the performance [against Everton]. I think it was an excellent performance and maybe it is the start of many, many victories…”

— Louis van Gaal, as quoted by reporters after United’s 3-0 win over Everton at Goodison Park

Manchester United manager Louis van Gaal’s suddenly-gained-and-recently-lost “happy feelings” have returned to a roaring welcome. The Dutchman has every reason to be happy with his team’s performance at Goodison Park this past Saturday. After what was nothing short of a disastrous performance last time out against Arsenal, United needed to respond positively and convincingly in order to put their title aspirations on track. The fact that the opponent was Everton and the match was to be played at Goodison Park didn’t exactly inspire pre-match confidence amongst the United faithful, especially when the Red Devils have never won there in three straight attempts in the past, let alone score a goal. Ultimately, that all changed on Saturday as Manchester United cruised to a 3-0 victory, rendering the pain of losing to Arsenal with the same scoreline obsolete and putting their ambitious title hunt back on track.

Sometimes, all it takes is a tough loss to jolt awake players and managers alike, especially on a team like United. In this case, the crushing loss to Arsenal serves even more, seeing that it completely exposed the shaky game-plan concocted by Louis van Gaal since the beginning of the season. True enough, in order to solicit a response, critical changes were made in the match against Everton. Whatever went wrong against Arsenal was rectified at Goodison Park.

A number of players were restored to the Starting XI in place of others who disappointed at the Emirates. Marcos Rojo, Phil Jones, Ander Herrera and Morgan Schneiderlin were all given starts in place of Daley Blind, Michael Carrick, Ashley Young and Memphis Depay. With these changes in personnel, for the first time ever this season, Manchester United looked more balanced and solid as a unit. Chris Smalling finally has a natural partner at centre back in Phil Jones instead of makeshift ones like Daley Blind. Marcos Rojo excelled at left back, translating his form from Argentina colors into United colors.

If this continues, the Red Devils won’t miss Luke Shaw that much, plus they won’t get to see Ashley Young at left-back ever again. Schneiderlin’s hustle and bustle provided much needed mobility in the center of the field, complementing Bastian Schweinsteiger well. In fact, his style will complement any of the midfielders available at United. Therefore, making him the immovable piece in midfield, something which van Gaal should have been doing in the first place, can become the Dutchman’s wisest move. And finally, we have the star of the show, Ander Herrera, who provided the spark for United’s perfect response with a goal and an assist.

“If we keep playing like this we can beat everybody. We showed everyone that Manchester United are here and that we are here to fight for everything. When you lose the way that we did against Arsenal then we couldn’t wait for this game. I think the first 20 minutes today were fantastic and this was the way that we had to react…”

— Ander Herrera, speaking to MUTV post-match

Indeed, the reaction was superb. Confidence was not only restored, it was reinforced with the staunch self-belief that they can beat anybody on this current level of performance. Note that this is not over-confidence on their part. Notice that the statement made by van Gaal and Herrera all preached a bit of cautiousness, with the former sounding optimistic for the future. That these statements were made in the aftermath of a convincing victory underlines the character and mental strength of the Red Devils.

Amidst all the talk of positive reaction and return to form, the changes made by Louis van Gaal also meant that a few players were left out. Memphis Depay and Daley Blind were condemned to the bench while Ashley Young didn’t even make the squad. Michael Carrick was also on the bench, but at least he made a substitute appearance. While the changes have been good for United (in this match at least), the names dropped were significant names, particularly Memphis Depay.

While Daley Blind’s best position may need to be sorted out by the manager and coaches at United (this could take a while), it is Memphis Depay’s form that is worrying. At Goodison Park, he saw his starting position being taken over by Anthony Martial, with Wayne Rooney leading the line and Ander Herrera pulling the strings behind. In fact when push came to shove, Louis van Gaal opted to sub in Jesse Lingard for Juan Mata instead of Depay in the second half. This may be viewed as a loss of faith by the manager in the young attacker, but van Gaal insisted that this wasn’t the case.

“Do I still believe in him? Of course. The problem with young players is that they’re not consistent. That you have to learn. We have to give him time…”

— Louis van Gaal, when asked by the press about Memphis Depay

Based on the statement, Depay will be given time to correct his deficiencies and adapt to the tactical approach and the rigors of the Premier League. He is after all, lest we forget, only 21 years old. Whatever it is that ails him, United will need him more than ever given that they have a Champions League match at Arena Khimki against CSKA Moscow mid-week before they ring in the Manchester Derby against Manchester City at Old Trafford in the space of four days. With the time constraints and the long-distance travel between Manchester and Moscow set to play a huge role in proceedings, expect Depay and the others dropped against Everton to feature heavily in at least one, if not both, of those matches.

Having come up with the right response after the Emirates debacle, United will look to a more challenging week ahead. In fact, this week could make or break United’s season both in the Champions League and in the English Premier League. Louis van Gaal will hope to tease more of the same from his squad, provided that he manages to balance things out at Moscow and at home.
