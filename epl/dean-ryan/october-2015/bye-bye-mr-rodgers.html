Bye Bye, Mr. Rodgers

It’s adios Brendan Rodgers as Liverpool awaits the arrival of Jürgen Klopp.

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

It was only a matter of time, really. If you ask me, this should have happened back in the summer, but somehow, Brendan Rodgers passed the club’s assessment back then. Heck, he was even allowed to spend 80 million pounds on new players. Now, eight games into the new season, after recording only 3 wins, Liverpool’s owners, Fenway Sports Group, finally had enough. Was it an untimely firing, though?

"It hasn't gone well for [Rodgers] this season. He needed to make a good start to this season. Because he was maybe fortunate in terms of staying on at the end of last season. A lot of his staff moved on, new staff came in. He spent £70m to £80m, then seven (eight) games later Liverpool are changing a manager. That's what I don't like about that. Just do it in the summer if that's the situation…”

— Jamie Carragher, Liverpool star and Sky Sports Analyst

Former Liverpool star Jamie Carragher, who now works as a pundit for Sky Sports, seemed to share the same sentiments as myself regarding the timing of Rodgers' firing. But, then again, if you think about it, Rodgers’ departure may be the next best thing timing-wise. We are only eight games into the season, so there’s still plenty of time for the new manager to steady the ship and bring Liverpool to a respectable league finish. Then, there’s the Europa League, which the new manager can take seriously enough to actually go deep into the tournament, perhaps even winning it outright. Add to this revamped outlook this upcoming international break, and we have the perfect scenario for a new manager to come in and become acquainted with everything Liverpool.

Why the optimism? Because the incoming manager, although not yet confirmed, is rumored to be the charismatic and highly-touted Jürgen Klopp. The man behind the successful Borussia Dortmund side, who challenged the established order in the German Bundesliga and Europe, is now set to take the reins at Anfield and bring his unique brand of soccer to the English Premier League. It is not a done deal yet, but when the list of other candidates consists of someone who just had back surgery (looking at you, Carlo Ancelotti), contracted to another club (Frank de Boer), and unproven credentials (Walter Mazzarri), it is hard not to believe the developments surrounding #KloppToTheKop.

Liverpool, a club as seeped in English football lore as they come, is about to turn hipster as soon as Klopp sets a foot in Anfield. A change in tactics, culture, and mentality are all on the cards at Anfield. It’s safe to say that we may never see the 3-5-2 again. That should be a relief for the Kop, seeing how uninspired and ineffective that formation has truly been. For me, the 3-5-2 only works in Italy and, if you want to actually win something with it, one only needs to take a look at the Andrea Pirlo-led Juventus team of the past few seasons.

With no 3-5-2 formation at play, this means Emre Can will be freed from playing in central defense. He’ll probably be slotted in midfield, his natural and favored position on the pitch.

This brings me to Klopp’s probable formation at Liverpool. If his tactics at Dortmund are any indication, a high tempo 4-2-3-1 is to be expected. How the current personnel at Liverpool will fit into this tactical change is a relative unknown, but if any manager can assuage this revamping, it’s Herr Klopp.

If Klopp’s tinkering fails in the next few months, then there’s always the January transfer window to rely on in order to fix the glaring weakness (if there are any). But first, he needs to ensure there will be no interference from the club’s so-called “transfer committee.” One could argue that a contributing factor in Brendan Rodgers’ demise at Liverpool this inefficient system of handling transfers. Therefore, Klopp needs to make sure that he has full, absolute control on any transfer deals that need to be made during his tenure.

Still, all of this is just hypothetical. There are a whole host of possibilities in store at Anfield this week, but the genius that is Jürgen Klopp is responsible for almost all of those. Such is the aura of this man. And, that bubbling positivity is beginning to creep in, nay, beginning to envelop the Reds and their fandom. Usually, when a manager gets fired, it is a low point for a club, with the uncertainty bringing copious amounts of fear into the equation for fans. But not this time. Not at Liverpool.

As for the man who was fired (Brendan Rodgers), he can opt for a quick turnaround if he’s willing to take the Sunderland job that Dick Advocaat vacated this past weekend. Or, if he wants some time off, then further managerial openings are expected in the future, namely at Newcastle, Aston Villa, or the 16th-place Chelsea (yikes)! But one thing is for certain though, he won’t be going to Arsenal. That job will be Arsène Wenger’s till the day he walks away.

But Le Professeur had some words of encouragement for the repudiated Rodgers:

“I think he was very unlucky not to win the title - he was the closest of all their managers. I wish him good luck.”

— Arsène Wenger on Brendan Rodgers’ firing

Farewell, Brendan Rodgers.

Ryan Dean
