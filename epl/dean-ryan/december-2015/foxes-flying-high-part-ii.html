Foxes Flying High - Part II

Jamie Vardy’s scoring streak is over, but Riyad Mahrez’s hat-trick ensured Leicester’s 3-0 victory over Swansea

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

It’s December already, which means that it’s that time of the year where everything gets a bit hectic. With the holidays around the corner, people are rushing to get things done, especially when it comes to shopping. And that’s just in Malaysia! The English Premier League is similarly on the verge of entering the “real” business period of matches.

The dreaded congested fixture list of December has been a staple of the English Premier League at a time when other major leagues across Europe take this opportunity to hibernate in the form of a winter break. While the pros and cons of having a winter break will continue to rumble on behind the scenes, the well-oiled machine of the league will proceed as planned, accompanied with the usual fanfare and excitement from fans all across the globe. The same can’t be said for the clubs participating, though.

Leicester City enters this dreaded period with a sense of optimism. On top of the league after their comprehensive 3-0 victory over Swansea, one would be forgiven to think that Leicester are in over their heads, having no business to be where they are currently. By that assumption, one could say the same about Chelsea: José Mourinho’s dithering Blues have no business being in the wrong half of the table with the amount of “star” players on their roster. But number one the Foxes are, and they are looking primed to consolidate their position based on their performance against the Swans.

“I don’t change my first goal, my ambition is very high, but my goal is that. Next matches against Chelsea, Everton, Liverpool, [Manchester] City - not bad.”

— Leicester manager Claudio Ranieri, when asked if the target of reaching 40 points would be altered

“Modesty” is the buzzword around King Power these days, as evidenced by their manager’s assertion that their target of reaching 40 points remains their biggest objective of the season. Call it unambitious perhaps, but Ranieri is doing wonders for the club’s psyche here, keeping the team grounded and not caught up in the moment. After all, we are looking at a team who made it through last season by collecting a paltry sum of 41 points. Therefore, it is only sensible for them to reach that checkpoint first before moving on to something much more ambitious, like winning the English Premier League title outright.

What is notable from Ranieri’s statement is the fixture list for Leicester for the rest of the month. That list is just short of a murderer’s row of opponents for the Foxes. Even by discounting the dysfunctional state of affairs at Chelsea, the fixture list is looking every bit as one that makes or breaks a title-chasing side. And, no matter what the manager says they’re going to do or how they will approach things, Leicester is a title-chasing football club right now. Not to mention, they are in possession of a healthy, competitive squad, spearheaded by their dynamic duo, Jamie Vardy and Riyad Mahrez.

The scoring streak might have ended for Jamie Vardy at the Liberty Stadium, but, unlike The Undertaker’s Wrestlemania streak ending, there’s nothing sad and shocking about his inability to score in the match. If it wasn’t for Łukasz Fabiański’s presence in goal for Swansea, Vardy would have at least scored a goal, but it just wasn’t meant to be. Leicester fans don’t need to worry about their top striker, though, considering his work rate is as top notch as ever. His awesome assist to teammate Riyad Mahrez for Mahrez’s hat-trick - instead of going for goal himself - highlights his selflessness and his perceived priority for the team over individual records.

Riyad Mahrez’s hat-trick, meanwhile, reminded everyone that he is as dangerous as his more illustrious teammate Jamie Vardy. While Vardy hogged the spotlight with his record-setting run, Mahrez was quietly going about his business down the right hand side for Leicester, lighting up opponents with his amazing dribbling skills. The same could be said of central midfielder N’Golo Kante and left back, Christian Fuchs. Both were summer acquisitions that have ingrained themselves successfully into the Leicester starting XI and acclimatized themselves to the rigors of the English Premier League.

All of this bodes well for Leicester’s chances for their upcoming fixtures. For starters, by hosting Chelsea at King Power Stadium next, they can begin to accumulate more points for their journey towards 40 points and beyond by handing the Blues their second straight defeat (Mourinho’s men lost to Bournemouth this past weekend). While their opponents are in a quandary right now - being outright dysfunctional - and being the subject of much ridicule in the media, Ranieri would do well to remind his players not to underestimate the defending champions (I still can’t believe we’re still calling them this). However, reigning in their ambition and galvanizing their confidence shouldn’t be a problem for these high-flying Foxes.
