The Toon Breaks the Streak

Newcastle overcome Spurs 2-1 at White Hart Lane to end Tottenham’s 15-game unbeaten streak

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

“Streak” seems to be the buzzword in form over this past weekend. Be it by winning or by being undefeated over a certain amount of matches, fans will be impressed with the proceedings nonetheless. What captures more attention, though, is when the streak is finally broken. Die-hard fans will fall to their knees in despair, shocked and sad at the fate that befell them. Cynics, critics, and opposition fans will mock and jeer them relentlessly while at the same time rejoicing and, perhaps, sneaking in a laugh or two. ‘I told you so’ or ‘I knew this would happen’ are just some of the phrases that will get thrown out a lot in the midst of the ensuing endless banter. Hopefully, no one gets hurt along the way.

Over the weekend, a few notable streaks were on the line. Some of these were broken. And some still continue to roll ahead full steam. The Carolina Panthers continued their winning streak in the NFL. They now tower over the league with a perfect 13-0 record. Paris St. Germain continued their dominance in Ligue 1 by remaining undefeated. Clearly, fans of both of these juggernauts relished in yet another victorious weekend. But what of the fans of the Golden State Warriors, whose 24-game winning streak was broken by the Milwaukee Bucks? What of Chris Weidman, who was undefeated and has beaten legends like Anderson Silva, Vitor Belfort, and Lyoto Machida, but lost his UFC Middleweight title to Luke Rockhold via TKO at UFC 194?

“They’ll bounce back” is what fans would say, be it the hardcore few or the casual majority. Indeed, in looking at the Golden State Warriors and Chris Weidman - taking into account the amount of talent and their relatively young age - it’s a foregone conclusion that they will bounce back. Different dynamics are at play between the two sports, most notably when answering the question of not if, but when they’ll bounce back. The Warriors can bounce back almost immediately due to their NBA scheduling (their next game is against the lowly 11-14 Phoenix Suns). The Weidman situation is more nuanced: He will need to rest, recuperate from any injuries sustained, retrain himself again, and then, finally, we’ll get to see him in action again in a few months time.

Unlike Weidman, Tottenham Hotspur won’t need that long or too short a time to rectify their shocking capitulation at the hands of the Toon - they have a whole week to rebound - but still, I have to say I didn’t see this coming. Newcastle were, after all, mired in the relegation zone - or, as I like to call it, the Chelsea ranch - heading into the Sunday clash with Spurs. After this victory, they are now in 15th place with 16 points, leapfrogging Chelsea along the way. Meanwhile Spurs are stuck in 5th, level on points with Crystal Palace at 26 points apiece. With this loss, Spurs’ unimpressive 15 game unbeaten streak comes to an abrupt end. Yes, “unimpressive” because the streak consisted of more draws than wins (eight draws compared to six wins).

But where Claudio Ranieri, Roberto Martínez, Manuel Pellegrini, Arsène Wenger, Jürgen Klopp, and José Mourinho failed, Newcastle’s Steve McClaren succeeded: Newcastle, of all teams, were the ones lucky enough to break Spurs’ unbeaten streak. Even taking into account his side’s other shocking victory - the 2-0 win against Liverpool last week - I still wouldn’t have picked Newcastle to win against Spurs, at White Hart Lane no less. The manner in which they achieved the victory over the Lilywhites was very impressive, too. Heading into the halftime break, they were already behind 1-0 thanks to Eric Dier. But they, to quote fans, “bounced back” in the second half, spurred by (no pun intended) goals from Aleksandar Mitrović and Ayoze Pérez. Although the winning goal was partly Hugo Lloris’s fault, the fightback displayed from the Toon was something not usually associated with them.

“Uncharacteristic” would be the correct term to describe Newcastle’s performances as of late. This sudden upturn in fortunes can be traced all the way back to their 5-1 drubbing at the hands of Alan Pardew’s Crystal Palace side. It’s as if that thrashing has awoken something in them that allows performances like the ones against Liverpool and Spurs to come out. Perusing the names in the squad, the Toon possess some of the more precocious talents in the league. Players like Moussa Sissoko, Georginio Wijnaldum, Daryl Janmaat and the now-injured Tim Krul are high-quality players, ably supported by what some would consider a decent Premier League squad. Therefore, performances like these should actually be the expectation instead of the exception.

“You know at Newcastle you’re only two days away from another crisis, so we will keep our feet on the ground.”

— Newcastle manager Steve McLaren, addressing the media after the 2-1 win against Spurs

Based on the manager’s assertions, I can’t help but feel relieved. Relieved that the team at least knows how unpredictable they can be. Although the vagueness on what actually caused this unpredictability is a huge problem for them. It is something that has been bothering them for years, making the team somewhat of an enigma despite the high quality of players going through the door at St. James’s Park. Accordingly, McLaren is right to make certain that his team plants its feet firmly on the ground, especially as they approach the festive fixture list. Until the real problem is found, they could do worse than just focusing on the next task at hand. And for now, that task is hosting Aston Villa at home.

In what could be an important match in the fight to stave off relegation, the timing is just right for Newcastle to build on their current winning streak. Victory against the Villans will ensure that they take the much-needed points against a direct relegation-threatened candidate. Sorry Newcastle fans, that is the reality of your club right now, a must-win game against a relegation-threatened candidate. And you deserve better. But for now, I encourage you to show up in force at the stadium, cheer the team on, and enjoy the moment. Besides, who knows? The club could be on the verge of going on an historic undefeated streak. Crazier things have happened this season.

Ryan Dean
