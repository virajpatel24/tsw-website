Nothing to be Ashamed of

Spurs earn a creditable goalless draw against Chelsea at White Hart Lane

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

“Boring.” It’s a justifiable tag given the circumstances in place when Spurs drew 0-0 against Chelsea at White Hart Lane. In a weekend where Palace thrashed Newcastle 5-1 and Bournemouth played to a highly entertaining 3-3 draw with Everton, the goalless draw between the Lilywhites and the Blues is definitely qualifies as boring. Yet, this label is still unfair given that Spurs gave everything they had against their defensive-minded West London opponents, who started the match without a true striker (Diego Costa was benched).

Going into the match, one would be easily forgiven for expecting an offensive shootout to take place. After all, these two sides did play in a highly entertaining affair last January that ended with Spurs walking out 5-3 winners. Of course, that Spurs victory, came against a soon-to-be crowned champion Chelsea. This time around, the Blues are languishing near the relegation zone, mired in controversy after controversy, and, quite frankly, really unstable. Mauricio Pochettino, on the other hand, oversees a North London side that is on an unparalleled undefeated streak since the opening day loss to Manchester United. The Argentinian’s side also has the most shots on target so far this season with 88; hence, Chelsea were ripe for the taking. However, there was a catch.

“We need energy because it’s a Sunday morning, flying back from Azerbaijan on Thursday and no day to prepare for the game.”

— Mauricio Pochettino, on his side’s potential lack of fitness heading into the Chelsea match

Tottenham had just returned from a whopping 5,000+ mile trip to Azerbaijan in a match against Qarabag in the Europa League. They won 1-0 and will advance to the knockout stages. But the win far from home came at a heavy cost.

“Maybe we lacked a bit of freshness at the end to create some extra chances. It was a hard trip to play Qarabag but, like I said before, we didn’t have an excuse for freshness today, and I feel it a bit to be honest.”

— Jan Vertonghen, after the match against Chelsea

For a team that is supposedly lacking in freshness and devoid of energy, Spurs acquitted themselves well throughout the Chelsea match. They actually had four shots on target, courtesy of attempts by Harry Kane (one), Heung-min Son (two) and Moussa Dembélé (one). Chelsea, in comparison, only sneaked a lone shot on target through their out-of-position, false-nine forward Eden Hazard. If anything, the Blues were the ones who were playing like they just got back from Azerbaijan as they sat back and invited pressure before they unleashed spurts of a measly counterattack. At least, by playing like this, the Blues continued their status as a relegation-threatened club while Spurs continued looking more and more like a title-chasing club in both the Premier League and in Europe.

“We played the defending champions and it is good that you get the feeling that Chelsea was a small team and Tottenham feel they can win every game.”

— Mauricio Pochettino, on Chelsea after the match

Indeed they could have won the game, if not for the untimely departure of Ryan Mason early in the second half through injury. That pivotal moment changed the complexion of the game tactically for Spurs, with the excellent Moussa Dembélé moving to a deeper position to compensate for Mason’s injury-enforced absence. Prior to that, Dembélé had been busy weaving through the Nemanja Matić-Cesc Fàbregas duo with relative ease, helping to unsettle the fragile Chelsea defense that was clearly lacking the hulking, taskmaster figure of John Terry. This, in turn, helped free up space for the attacking triumvirate of Christian Eriksen, Son, and Kane in the final third of the pitch, with the latter two coming the closest to scoring. However, since Mason was forced out with injury and Spurs began to feel the late fatigue setting in, a win was not meant to be.

To think that Chelsea could have won the game from their one and only shot on target - courtesy of Eden Hazard - is simply indescribable at best. Thankfully, Hugo Lloris was there to make an excellent reaction stop, effectively ensuring that Spurs walked away still unbeaten in 13 matches, which is a new record for the storied North London club. So while the spotlight shines upon Chelsea and their never-ending controversies - the latest being Diego Costa’s omission and bib-throwing at José Mourinho - the Lilywhites are quietly continuing to build on their unbeaten run that they hope will lead them towards the summit, both in the Premier League and the Europa League.

Despite their unbeaten run, Spurs are still outside the coveted top four positions in the Premier League, having accumulated the most draws in the league with seven. However, they are just four points behind joint leaders, Manchester City and Leicester, and two points behind fellow North London rivals, Arsenal. This could all change, of course. With December rolling around, the dreaded congested holiday fixtures will rear their ugly heads. Considering the vibrant performance they displayed against Chelsea after a 5000+ mile trip midweek, Spurs fans won’t have to worry one bit, especially with the low quality of opposition the club will face in December. Starting with West Brom next, the road to the top begins now. Come On You Spurs!
