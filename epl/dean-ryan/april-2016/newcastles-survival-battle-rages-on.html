Newcastle’s Survival Battle Rages On

Jack Colback’s equalizer in the 2-2 draw against Liverpool ensured Newcastle’s safety for another day

by Ryan Dean

Share Tweet

Just a couple more games to go in the English Premier League and a few nerve-wrecking storylines are still ongoing. There’s the enthralling Leicester vs. Tottenham fight for the title. Manchester City and Arsenal duking it out for third place, and an automatic UEFA Champion’s League qualification spot. There’s a mad scramble for the Europa League places between Manchester United, West Ham United, Southampton and Liverpool. And of course, the dreaded relegation battle also wages on. Dumpster dwellers Aston Villa are already doomed, but which two of the other three clubs accompany them in the relegation zone when this season takes its final breath? Right now, it’s a three-way battle between Sunderland, Norwich and Newcastle.

This close battle between the three clubs could very well be decided on the final matchday. If only there was a simple solution to spare us all of the emotional wreck and anxieties of the final few matches. In my wrestling-loving head, the perfect solution would be to set up a triple-threat match between “Big” Sam Allardyce, Alex “Mr. Clean” Neil, and Rafa “Undertaker” Benítez at the upcoming WWE pay-per-view Payback. Definitely way better than a run-of-the-mill Roman Reigns match, right?

But, this is football, and not the fantasy world of the WWE, so we do have to endure all the edgy emotions for the next few weeks, along with the potential heartbreak and disappointment. For those whose chosen club survives, the feelings of relief and joy will keep their hearts happy till the Euros begin this summer.

All of the glitz and glamor of the Premier League title is enveloped in a classic tale of survival. The theory of evolution teaches us that only the strongest survive. The strongest clubs routinely win the league title. But that’s not survival, is it? True survival lies in the stories and broken dreams of the Premiership’s bottom feeders. Those lucky few who just want to survive. To survive in the brutal Premiership, reaching the 40-point mark is key. Failure to do that with a few games left can force these teams to participate in a relegation dogfight, similar to the one we’re witnessing currently.

Newcastle are no strangers to relegation fights. In fact, they were relegated back in 2009. However, they quickly bounced back into the Premier League at the first opportunity. It won’t be simple as back then if they get relegated this time around. The competition in the Championship is significantly tougher nowadays, and bouncing back immediately is way more challenging than surviving in the English Premier League. Just ask QPR, Fulham, and Cardiff.

Perhaps it was that sobering fact in mind that compelled Newcastle owner Mike Ashley (pictured below) to fire Steve McClaren last month, a move that was deemed a bit late considering the dire situation at the club. Yet, in came his replacement, Rafa Benítez, with a contract reminiscent of a bounty hunter, taking advantage of a desperate man’s plight. The image of a gaffer interested only in the riches while doing his reputation no harm was conjured, yet, the performances of the team do nothing to suggest that at all.

After a sort of bedding in period after his appointment as manager, the last three games for Newcastle has been nothing short of great for the team. A 3-0 victory over Swansea was followed by consecutive draws against top opposition in the likes of Manchester City and, of course, Liverpool. Certainly, it can be argued that the draws came courtesy of their opponent’s travails in Europe, but for a club fighting for survival, every point counts.

“Yes, I think we can stay up. It depends on the other teams too but if we can fight with this spirit we can do it.”

— Rafa Benítez, talking to reporters after the Liverpool match

It is hard to overlook the confidence and fight that is evidenced right now in the Newcastle squad. Benítez has got the team believing that they can survive-and-advance against the big teams now. Of course, we are talking about a man whose legacy is based on that one famous night in Istanbul. The stage is different now, but important nonetheless. The “fighting back when all seems lost” mantra is now being embedded in the Newcastle players, and recent results have proved that it’s having the desired effect.

Moussa Sissoko is now playing like the player who qualified to represent France in the 2014 World Cup. Andros Townsend is running on the right side, like he’s making up for lost time back at Spurs. The fulcrum of the Newcastle defense looks settled now, with the combination of Chancel Mbemba and Jamaal Lascelles impressing. Vurnon Anita has been a revelation since being shifted to the right back position from his “natural” midfield position. All in all, the whole team is looking good and motivated to survive.

“We just need to keep on going. We’re not there yet, we know that. We need to take it step by step, game by game, and work hard like the last two games and it will come for us.”

— Vurnon Anita, speaking with nufcTV prior to the Liverpool match

Game-by-game is the right mindset to take in this situation, especially with the intricacies surrounding the relegation battle. The final three matches for the Geordies will be Crystal Palace at home, followed by the already-relegated Aston Villa away, and then the season will come to an end with a match against the title-seeking Spurs at home. Palace and Villa are certainly conquerable for Newcastle, but the final match with Tottenham presents somewhat of a mystery. If the Premier League title is already decided by then, Tottenham will probably field a sub-par side. If the title is still on the line, expect nothing but wave after wave of onslaught from the Lilywhites.

Results elsewhere will also affect the outcome for Newcastle. Sunderland and Norwich both have a game in hand; however, both will have to deal with challenging opponents. Still, the Magpies will do best to just focus on their own path, controlling what they can control. “The Miracle at Newcastle” could still be a thing. Who knows? Newcastle could be what Leicester have been this season, contending for the Premier League title and qualifying for the Champions League. Maybe Andros Townsend will be crowned Player of the Year next season. If that happens, then finally, England will have it’s own “Juventus.” Miracle, right?
