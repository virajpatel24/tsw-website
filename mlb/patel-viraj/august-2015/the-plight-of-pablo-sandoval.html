The Plight of Pablo Sandoval

In a downtrodden 2015 MLB season, the Boston Red Sox player is left without many hits, home runs, and, most importantly, supporters.

by Viraj Patel

Share Tweet

As Major League Baseball prepares to transition into the final third of the 2015 season, the San Francisco Giants are probably counting their blessings right about now. They’re coming off of a triumphant 2014 season that saw them crowned as World Series champions for the third time in the last five years. Their pitching staff, led by southpaw ace Madison Bumgarner, has been largely successful thus far. And, their infield, comprising of Brandon Belt (1B), Joe Panik (2B), Brandon Crawford (SS), and Matt Duffy (3B), has become one of the best and youngest in baseball. Finally, all-star catcher Buster Posey rounds out the team nicely with his leadership and talent.

This Giants team currently sits in second place (59–49) in the NL West behind their rivals, the Los Angeles Dodgers (62–46). They’ve also fared second or third in the NL wild card race throughout the season. But, this shouldn’t worry the Giants since they made the World Series (and won) as a wild card team last season, too. For all intents and purposes, the Bay Area Giants have performed as expected (with some room for improvement) and are on pace to make another postseason run.

Such cannot be said, however, for the 2013 World Series champions, the Boston Red Sox. The Sox grudgingly sit in dead last in the AL East. They nurse a dreadful 48–61 record and have been out of the playoffs conversation for a while now. Succinctly put, everything that can possibly go wrong for the Red Sox has now gone wrong.

The centerpiece of this New England calamity has been the substandard performances of the Red Sox’s third baseman, Pablo Sandoval. As of Thursday, August 7th, Sandoval is batting 0.260 with 9 HRs, 35 RBI, and a WAR of -0.4. Sandoval’s defensive prowess, usually the cherry-on-top in his prior seasons, has also been just as disheartening to watch.

To cap it all off, Sandoval, who signed a five-year, $95 million contract with the Sox this season after spending his first seven seasons in San Francisco, has been thoroughly replaced in the hearts and minds of his Giants fans since the rookie, Matt Duffy, has been on fire the entire season for the team. Duffy, who is only 24-years-old, sports a 0.309 batting average while amassing 9 HRs, 51 RBI, and 108 hits. Additionally, Duffy’s defensive output has also been far superior to that of Sandoval’s this season. And, the best part is Duffy will only cost the Giants $509,000 this season while Pablo Sandoval will be paid $17.60 million by the Red Sox.

Clearly, the underachieving Red Sox have been the focal point of New England’s ire these past few weeks. Sandoval, Hanley Ramírez, and the rest of the underperforming Sox players have been in the fans’ line-of-fire at various points.

“It’s bad, bro. I don’t think I ever remember coming in [Anaheim] and losing a whole series like this, every game….This year is just a bad year, man. I’ve seen things here in 13 years I’ve never seen. Bro, it’s just like we can’t find a way.”

— David Ortiz, Red Sox DH, after suffering a sweep by the LA Angels

To make matters worse, “Panda” Sandoval was suspended for a game earlier in the season by the team for going on Instagram during a game and “liking” two photos of a female Instagram user. Really, Panda??? You couldn’t wait till after the game?

Clearly, this team is light-years away from the 2013 team that won the World Series. Red Sox GM Ben Cherington equally has no earthly idea how to go about fixing this team before it’s too late. And, it’s already too late.

Some of the Boston media have also called for the immediate trades of both Sandoval and Hanley Ramirez.

“But it should scare you that these young players are working for the same company as Ramirez and Sandoval, two overpriced, underperforming veterans who are just going along for the ride.

It hasn’t worked out for these guys in Boston.

They need to go.”

— Steve Buckley, Boston Herald, "Hanley Ramirez and Pablo Sandoval must go"

Sandoval’s girth also received some publicity during his Giants days from time to time. During Spring Training this season, Steve Silva tweeted out a photo of Sandoval doing some defensive drills while sporting a hefty belly.

For the most part, despite his occasional weight troubles, Sandoval put up some great numbers for the Giants, especially in the postseason. And, these performances, combined with the Giants’ postseason successes, were usually enough to deter the critics. But, this season has proved that any indiscretion (his Instagram use during a game) and downturns (woeful offensive and defensive stats) won’t be tolerated by the Boston fans and media.

The only way for Pablo Sandoval to remain in Boston for the duration of his five-year deal?

Start putting up 2009 and 2011-type numbers. Start helping his Red Sox team. But, most importantly, start acting like he cares.

UPDATE (Wednesday, August 19th, 2015): As of this update, the Red Sox are still bottom-dwellers in the AL East. Yesterday, the team also announced a massive front office restructuring that will oust GM and vice-president Ben Cherington. The big-money free agent duo of Hanley Ramirez and Pablo Sandoval sport the third-worst and second-worst WARs of this season. Check out this great Jonah Keri piece on Grantland released today as it goes over the factors that led to the Red Sox’s deplorable season up to this point.
