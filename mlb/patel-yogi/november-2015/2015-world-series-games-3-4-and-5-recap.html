2015 World Series - Games 3, 4 and 5 Recap

The Kansas City Royals emerge as the 2015 World Series champions

by Yogi Patel

Share Tweet

What a crazy World Series! We had two games that went into extra innings, and four nail-biters. The Royals showed the same type of resiliency they had displayed all year, and the Mets faltered after having a week of rest before beginning their World Series campaign.

David Murphy struggled mightily after taking ace after ace deep during the NLDS and NLCS. Baseball writers predicted that he was bound to regress to the mean eventually, but none of us expected that sudden drop-off would arrive in the freaking World Series. He ended up hitting a measly .150 in the Fall Classic. But it wasn’t just Murphy that struggled at the plate; the Mets couldn’t get anything going against the Royals. The Royals’ starters were effective for the first six innings in each game, and then the bullpen came in and shut down the Mets. The Mets, on the other hand, struggled when closing out games. Jeurys Familia, who had been dominant the coming into the World Series, proceeded to blow three save opportunities that all led to losses for the Mets.

Johnny Cueto was dominant in Game 2. Edinson Volquez was amazing despite pitching Game 5 after returning from the Dominican Republic after attending his father’s funeral.

The Royals hitters deserve all of the credit, though. Like I said before, they were able to play small ball and put together great at-bats to scratch across enough runs. The Royals showed that they were the better club in all facets. They showed that they could win with starting pitching, bullpen, timely hitting, and spectacular baserunning.

The Mets presented a tough challenge, but the Royals put together great at-bats against the flamethrowers the Mets threw at them. Good pitching beats good hitting, but small ball beats good pitching. The Kansas City Royals just proved that. They traveled a long way to capture their first Fall Classic since 1985. Congratulations go out to the 2015 Kansas City Royals.

But this World Series season-ending recap wouldn’t be complete without my recognizing two other Hall-of-Fame caliber talents making their presence felt during this year’s Fall Classic. Of course, I am speaking of Alex Rodriguez and Pete Rose. The 40-year-old A-Rod and the 74-year-old Rose were the delightful surprises of Fox’s World Series coverage. Both legends of the game are well-known for their transgressions – A-Rod has a past mired in steroid usage while Pete Rose was infamously banned from baseball after a gambling scandal in the mid-1980s.

MLB’s A-Rod and Pete have become the leading competitors to the NBA's Shaq-Charles Barkley duo as sports broadcasting’s most lovable, hilarious, and insightful tandem. During rain delays throughout the postseason, the A-Rod-Uncle Pete relationship kept us entertained as Rodriguez provided his insightful analysis of the game while Pete Rose did his best not to spew some asinine comment about race or gambling. None of us still know why this duo struck media gold this year, but we do know this: Pete Rose’s Fox salary was distributed only in scratch cards and lotto tickets.

Too soon?
