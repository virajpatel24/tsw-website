2015 AL Division Series Preview

Texas Rangers vs. Toronto Blue Jays; Houston Astros vs. Kansas City Royals

by Yogi Patel

Share Tweet

Texas Rangers vs. Toronto Blue Jays

Rogers Centre, Toronto, Canada

Thursday, October 8th

Game 1 - 3:30 PM EST on FS1

The Texas Rangers are the third team that overachieved this season (the first two were covered in my last column). They were not expected to be in the playoffs until next season, when Yu Darvish would make his return. However, trades that brought southpaw Cole Hamels and former catcher Mike Napoli to Arlington mid-season have re-inspired this team to play with a fiery demeanor.

The Rangers’ ALDS opponents, the Toronto Blue Jays, finally broke their 22-year drought of missing the postseason by securing the AL East divisional crown. The last time they featured in the postseason (1993), they went on to win the World Series against the Philadelphia Phillies. Although this club is built much differently than that 1993 squad was, a World Series appearance this year is not out of the equation.

Can the superstars dominate the postseason?

While the AL MVP frontrunner, Josh Donaldson, absolutely punished pitchers during the regular season and posted career highs in home runs (41), RBIs (123), doubles (41), slugging percentage (.371), and OPS (.939), he has not fared well in October baseball throughout his career. He has hit a paltry .233/.283/.256, with only one extra base hit – a double – in 43 career at-bats. It doesn’t get much better for the Blue Jays when you examine the other big names in their lineup. Troy Tulowitzki and Russell Martin both have sub-.220 career batting averages in the postseason.

Accordingly, it shouldn’t come as a surprise that all three of these hitters have more strikeouts than they do hits in their postseason careers. Donaldson has fanned 14 times, compared to only getting 10 base hits. Tulo has whiffed 17 times, compared to getting a base hit 12 times. And Martin has struck out 30 times, compared to getting 29 hits. These three superstars are the only position players with postseason experience on the entire Blue Jays offensive roster. Who even knows how sluggers José Bautista and Edwin Encarnación will fare?

Therefore, considering the quality of pitching on display from the Rangers this season, I do expect to see plenty of K’s in the box score, with the superstar Blue Jays hitters playing their part in the spectacle.

Is the Price right?

According to a timeless baseball adage, good pitching beats good hitting. In the postseason, this wisdom is even more appreciable. And that may pose a problem for the Blue Jays. Toronto’s ace David Price has terrible career postseason numbers. In ten games (only five starts), he has posted a 1-5 record with a 4.50 ERA over 40.0 innings. Additionally, no one will fear Mark Buehrle or R.A. Dickey given postseason baseball is about power pitching. If the Jays want to win, they need Price to pitch like he did in the regular season, if not better.

Can the Jays handle the pressure?

Simply put: There is a lot of pressure on this Toronto Blue Jays team. The club has the support of the entire nation of Canada, but that still may not be enough. But the greatest pressure will be on Roberto Osuna, their 20-year old rookie closer, to come in and get the biggest three outs each night he is called upon.

Tactically, the Jays made two blockbuster trades to acquire Tulowitzki and David Price mid-season, and they simply stole Donaldson from the A’s during the offseason. These aggressive team-building moves by GM Alex Anthopoulos gave their fan base hope that they would win the World Series this year.

But can they handle all of that pressure now that they are boiling in the pressure-cooker that is the MLB postseason?

My Prediction

No one expects the Rangers to make any noise this postseason -- that reminds me of the Royals last year. I think they will stun the baseball world and defeat the Blue Jays in four games (3-1).

Houston Astros vs. Kansas City Royals

Kauffman Stadium, Kansas City

Thursday, October 8th

Game 1 - 7:30 PM EST on FS1

The Astros are trying to be the 2015 version of the 2014 Royals. To do that, they must first beat the 2015 Royals in the ALDS. The Royals, on the other hand, got a taste of the Fall Classic last year, and are hungry for more.

The ‘Stros and the Royals are both young teams with copious amounts of talent. Southpaw Dallas Keuchel showed how effective he can be with only three days of rest as the Astros defeated the Yankees at Yankee Stadium in Tuesday night’s wild card game. Keuchel have even gone on to state that he feels like his pitching style is most effective when given short rest periods, which the playoffs always enforce on teams.

Will the Astros hitting take off?

The Astros only managed to get five hits against the Yankees, although two of them found the bleachers. They were 1-6 with RISP, and they were fanned nine times. However, they did draw five walks. Those RISP numbers will need to improve because they can’t count on the long ball to help them win every game, especially against a solid Royals pitching staff.

Can the Royals survive without Holland?

With closer Greg Holland having undergone Tommy John surgery earlier this month, Wade Davis moves into the closer’s role. Losing Holland hurts the Royals’ bullpen. They can’t end a game as quickly anymore. Last year, they were able to bring in Davis or Kelvin Herrera in the 6th or 7th inning to shutdown the opposition. This year, they will need someone else to step up, come in, and get crucial outs in the 6th or 7th inning.

Many times, games are won or lost long before the last three outs are made. For example, the Pirates’ best chance to win Wednesday’s wild card game came in the bottom of the 6th when they had bases loaded with only one out before Starling Marte grounded into a double play. I believe the Pirates lost the game on that play. With a strapped bullpen short on proven players, the Royals will need to ensure their pitching staff is able to ward off those Astros surges in the crucial 6th, 7th, and 8th innings.

My Prediction

In last year’s World Series, the Kansas City Royals were a “Madison-Bumgarner-performance-of-the-ages” away from winning their first World Series since 1985. “It’s the only thing that these players will think about -- getting back to this stage. Because of that, it’s going to serve them well going forward,” said general manager Dayton Moore. “Once you've tasted the playoffs and then the World Series and a Game 7, the only thing that motivates you is getting back on that platform. So it’s served us well.”

Believe me, the Royals are fueled and motivated for a World Series coronation in 2015. But, they will first need to beat the Astros in four games (3-1).
