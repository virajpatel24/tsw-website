2015 MLB Playoffs - Wild Card Preview

Astros vs. Yankees; Cubs vs. Pirates

by Yogi Patel

Share Tweet

AL Wild Card

Houston Astros vs. New York Yankees

Yankee Stadium, Bronx, New York

Tuesday, October 6th

8 PM EST on ESPN/WatchESPN

The Houston Astros are one of the three teams that, in my view, overachieved this season. I predicted in my last column that they would come back to win the AL West. However, the Texas Rangers got the better of them. Now, they have a date with the New York Yankees in a one game, winner-take-all, Wild Card showdown. Houston will send their rocket-man Dallas Keuchel to the mound with only three days of rest. The Bearded Beast that is Keuchel has had a career year in 2015 and will look to extend this new-found success deep into this Postseason.

The New York Yankees, who are returning to a familiar scene for the first time since 2012, will send Masahiro Tanaka to the mound.

I believe the key players for the Yankees will be Chris Young and John Ryan Murphy. Young is hitting .327/.397/.575 versus lefties this year. Keuchel, a dangerous southpaw himself, has limited left-handed hitters to a slash-line of .177/.201/.260. Yanks catcher John Ryan Murphy is hitting .266/.314/.456, while Greg Bird is hitting a measly .238/.347/.405. Carlos Beltran is hitting .255/.305/.447 against lefties this year. Beltran has more experience and is undoubtedly a proven postseason player, but if I were Joe Girardi, I would deploy Chris Young in right, Murphy at catcher, and move Brian McCann over to first.

Yes, the Yankees have a potentially dangerous lineup with Greg Bird and Carlos Beltran, but more often than not, it’s the role players that have much bigger impacts in the postseason. A recent example of this occurred in 2011 when St. Louis’ hometown hero David Freese showed up big-time over perennial living legend Albert Pujols, helping the Cards lift the World Series trophy that year.

Accordingly, the key for the Yankees winning lies in Chris Young and John Ryan Murphy.

Going into a hostile environment against a team with no prior postseason experience in the Astros usually does not end well. But, this one time, the Yankees will come away with a hard-fought 4-2 victory, with Chris Young delivering a key hit against Keuchel to help the Yanks win the game.

NL Wild Card

Chicago Cubs vs. Pittsburgh Pirates

PNC Park, Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania

Wednesday, October 7th

7:30 PM EST on TBS

The Chicago Cubs are the second of three teams that overachieved this year. I knew they would make the playoffs, but I had no idea they would win an astonishing 97 games! Cubs manager Joe Maddon deserves to be the NL Manager of the Year for leading such a young roster to the postseason. Many reporters and broadcasters often say that this Cubs team plays like “they don’t know they don’t belong in the playoffs just yet” – which is a good thing. It means that Maddon is ensuring that this team is playing to have fun, and winning games is just a result of that comforting environment.

This Wednesday night in Steel City, the Cubs will send the hottest pitcher in baseball, Jake Arrieta, to the mound. The Pittsburgh Pirates, despite winning 98 games, find themselves in familiar territory yet again. For the third year in a row, the Pirates find themselves hosting the Wild Card game. The Bucs will send their ace Gerrit Cole to the mound.

This will be a typical NL pitcher’s duel that die-hard fans, like myself, enjoy watching much more than the typical, universally appealing, high-scoring shootout. Both of these teams rank amongst the top ten in ERA, WHIP, and BAA. It should be no surprise that Arrieta and Cole are the main reasons behind their respective team’s pitching success.

Both pitchers will pitch as we expect; but Cole will make one big mistake and the Cubs’ sensational rookie Kris Bryant will energize Chicago, keeping their Cinderella Story alive. This game should be a fun one to watch. But, assuming Chicago pulls through, the NL Central showdown between the Cubs and Cardinals will be even greater fun for the viewing audience.

October baseball is finally here! Are you as excited as I am?
