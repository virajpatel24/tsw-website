2015 NL Championship Series Preview

Chicago Cubs vs. New York Mets

by Yogi Patel

Share Tweet

Chicago Cubs vs. New York Mets

Citi Field, Flushing, New York

Saturday, October 17th

Game 1 - 8:07 PM EST on TBS

Both of these teams put on amazing shows for their respective fan bases in their NLDS series. The Cubs did it by slugging home runs one after another, including Kyle Schwarber’s moonshot. And the Mets did it by outdueling Dodgers’ aces Zach Greinke and Clayton Kershaw. This NLCS matchup showcases two young, talented, but extremely different organizations. The Mets built their club around power pitching (Jacob deGrom, Matt Harvey, Noah Syndergaard, Steven Matz, and Zach Wheeler). The Cubs built their team around hitters (Kyle Schwarber, Anthony Rizzo, Jorge Soler, Kris Bryant, Addison Russell, and Javier Baez).

Power pitching vs. power hitting? (I’m in!)

The Cubs actually surprised me with how well they hit the Cardinals’ pitchers in the NLDS. But it shouldn’t come as a surprise since they’re so talented. They won’t have Russell for the series, but Baez has done a tremendous job filling in for him. He’s gone 4-5 including hitting a go-ahead 3-run shot in Game 4 to help them win the division series. Kyle Schwarber is just a beast out there. He’s showing his Popeye-like cartoonish power by hitting .538/.600/1.231, with three homers and five RBIs. More importantly, he’s shown great plate discipline, as evidenced by his three lone strikeouts thus far. Rizzo and Bryant are struggling through this postseason, but they have gotten hits (mostly clutch homers) when it has mattered the most. Even without the Rizzo-Bryant tandem firing on all cylinders, it’s still going to be difficult to slow these Cubs hitters down, so the Mets’ pitching will be extra-assertive in their quest to keep suppressing both Rizzo and Bryant. The Cubs, on the pitching front, have their 29-year-old ace Jake Arrieta to send out there, but Jon Lester and the rest of the staff have been relatively disappointing.

The Mets, on the other hand, feature young, power pitchers. Jacob deGrom outdueled Clayton Kershaw in Game 1 of the NLDS and then outdueled Zach Greinke in the most important start of his young career: the decisive Game 5 on Thursday night. Both starts came on the road and he only surrendered two earned runs in 13 innings and struck out 20 batters to boot. Syndergaard followed him with an impressive start in Game 2 and then came out of the bullpen and pitched phenomenally in Game 5. Both of them lit up the radar gun, so it should be fun to see them face these hitters. Matt Harvey might be the weakest link in this stacked Mets rotation, so the Cubs have to take full advantage when facing him.

A tale of two cities

A largely under-the-radar storyline in this series revolves around the cities themselves that will be cast under the limelight: Chicago and New York City. The glitz and glamor of NYC will face off against the blue-collar, Midwestern hustle of Chicago. It’s “The City That Never Sleeps” versus “The Windy City.” The city where “The Dark Knight” was filmed (Chicago) versus the city where “Taxi Driver” was filmed (New York). “Home Alone” versus “Home Alone 2: Lost in New York.” The Willis Tower versus the Empire State building. New York-style pizza versus the Chicago-style hot dog. O’Hare International Airport versus LaGuardia. The new, state-of-the-art Citi Field versus the currently-being-renovated Wrigley Field.

Those are all key points in play that will have no effect whatsoever on this seven game NLCS series. But it’s nice to have a refresher going in, right?

My prediction

In the NLDS match-up between the Mets and Dodgers, we saw a few classic clashes of elite pitchers facing off against each other. There were a ton of strikeouts and not that many runs scored, with Game 3 being the main exception. I think runs will be at a premium once again in the NLCS, especially when the Cubs have to face deGrom and Syndergaard, and strikeouts should help fill up the scorecards.

There are two movies in play here. In the blockbuster classic, “The Rookie of the Year,” the Cubs and Mets faced off in the NLCS and the Cubs ended up winning the World Series despite losing their magical rookie. Will their magic season, led by their talented rookies, share the same fate? Or will that magic falter, just like it did with Henry when he fell the second time?

The other movie is “Back to the Future Part II.” We all know how that ends up, with the Cubs winning the World Series with no twists or turns. So who will win? I have to stick with the old adage that good pitching beats good hitting, and the Mets have the pitching that can shutdown these Cubs. And, if nothing else, then we can all look forward to seeing how far Schwarber can launch one of deGrom’s triple-digit fastballs.

It will be a fun series that will give us great insights into these two young, talented teams that should face off plenty of times in the future. But this year, I have to stick with the Mets because of their pitching staff, although it’s frustrating to root against the Cubs and their Cinderella Story. Mets win in seven games.
