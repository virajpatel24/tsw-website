2015 World Series Preview

New York Mets vs. Kansas City Royals

by Yogi Patel

Share Tweet

New York Mets vs. Kansas City Royals

Kauffman Stadium, Kansas City, Missouri

Tuesday, October 27th

Game 1 - 8:07 PM EST on FOX

The lights are on. The stage is set. The players and coaches are ready. Are you ready? Because the Fall Classic is finally here! The New York Mets and the Kansas City Royals have both fought hard to reach the biggest stage there is in all of baseball. Who will go home with crowned as victors and known all across the baseball world as the best team in 2015?

Kansas City Royals

The Royals were eager to get back here and redeem themselves. Throughout the season, and especially now, many people have questioned whether Alex Gordon would have scored from third to tie the game in last year’s epic game seven. They Royals have a chance to give the media and fans something else to think about this year as they pursue their dreams of claiming the Fall Classic for the first time since 1985. They have a great chance to win it all.

New York Mets

No one expected the Mets to be here, let alone win the NL East. Even in their own division, everyone picked the star-studded Washington Nationals to win it all this year. But the Mets proved that games and divisions are won on the field and not on paper. So Bryce Harper, where’s your ring? There’s no denying the fact that this is one of the best and deepest rotations to pitch in the World Series.

Mets’ Starters vs. Royals’ Hitters

There’s no way to single out any matchups here. The Royals struck out the fewest times all season (973). So when I say that they had the highest contact rate, that shouldn’t come as a surprise. Well, they did have the highest contact rate, especially against fastballs. They will wait you out, just like they did to David Price. Yes, it may look like Price’s postseason woes continued, but against any other team, he would have gotten wins. But these Royals’ hitters just waited him out until his fastball started to weaken and then they piled up hit after hit. I expect them to do the same against the Mets’ young hurlers. The key to beating good pitching is small ball. There’s no team that does it better than the Royals. I expect to see a lot of good at-bats, making this a fun match-up to watch.

Daniel Murphy

Murphy has channeled his inner Babe Ruth, Barry Bonds, and Lou Gehrig this postseason. He and Lou Gehrig are the only players in history to get a hit, drive in a run, and score a run in seven straight postseason games. Joe Maddon also said that his streak is much more impressive than that of Bonds’ in 2002. He’s also homered in six consecutive postseason games, something that no major league baseball player – not even Babe Ruth or Hank Aaron – has done. Murphy is in some unchartered territory and he will look to keep that streak alive and help his team conquer their first championship since 1986. But will a week of rest throw his immaculate timing off?

Salvador Pérez vs. Mets’ Running Game

Mets could have run wild over the Cubs if they wanted to. No, not just because Jon Lester can’t throw to the bases, but because the Cubs aren’t that great at stopping the running game. However, Royals catcher Salvador Pérez can and will shut it down. The Mets did a great job of being aggressive and smart on the basepaths against the Dodgers and Cubs. Céspedes stealing third against the Cubs directly led to him scoring the go-ahead run on a pitch that got through the catcher. Don’t expect that to happen with Perez behind the plate.

Johnny Cueto

Cueto has been atrocious on the road this postseason. He has struggled since coming over into the AL from Cincinnati. But with the way that Yost has lined his rotation up, Cueto is in line to start Games 2 and 6, both at home. In the lone game he started against the Mets, which happened to be at Citi Field, he lost, despite tossing six innings of two run ball on two hits (one homer) and six strikeouts. I think that he might be better suited to start at Citi Field where he is more accustomed to navigating through a National League lineup. However, the Mets do not have a pure DH like the American League teams. Cueto will be very intriguing to watch given how polarizing his starts have been in the postseason this year.

Bartolo Colón

Colón has been great this postseason. But I believe that the Mets will need him to step up now more than ever. These Royals hitters will throw the Mets’ young hurlers into fits of frustration with their small ball play. Hence, Colón may have to come in to make a spot start if the Mets fall into an early hole, or may have to pitch more frequently to help bridge the gap between the starters and the back end of the bullpen. I think that he is the key piece for this Mets squad. If he can step up and deliver, whether on the field or behind the scenes in helping the pitchers out, the Mets can win.

My Prediction

This entire postseason I have been saying that good pitching always beats good hitting. But there is always a “Catch-22.” In this case, good pitching can be beat by small ball. Like I previously mentioned, there is no team that is better at small ball than the Kansas City Royals. I expect them to wait out the Mets’ tantalizing rotation and put the ball in play. With that being said, I do not think the Royals will take another heartbreak. They have worked far too hard and come too far to be sent home for the second straight year without a ring. It will be a great matchup, but the Kansas City Royals will claim the title in six games.
