2015 NL Division Series Preview

New York Mets vs. Los Angeles Dodgers; Chicago Cubs vs. St. Louis Cardinals

by Yogi Patel

Share Tweet

New York Mets vs. Los Angeles Dodgers

Dodger Stadium, Los Angeles, California

Friday, October 9th

Game 1 - 9:45 PM EST on TBS

The Mets find themselves playing in October for the first time since 2006, when they last won the NL East Crown. The Dodgers have won the NL West title the past three years, but haven’t exactly impressed in the postseason. Both teams feature power pitchers and, accordingly, we should see some great pitching performances.

Will Kershaw finally turn the corner?

It’s no secret that the best pitcher in baseball the past few years, Clayton Kershaw, has struggled greatly in the postseason – mainly against the Cardinals. He is 1-5 with a 5.12 ERA in 11 games (eight starts). Kershaw knows he needs to pitch better in these big games.

PHOTOGRAPH BY WWW.INSIDESOCAL.COM

But I have seen a different Kershaw this year – one that is driven to overcome his postseason failures of the past and continue his regular season dominance into the postseason. He has shown his frustration this season, but rather than shy away from it, he’s leaned into it. All of these signs are showing me that this year he will finally turn the corner and shake the proverbial money off his back.

How will this young Mets rotation fare in their first taste of the playoffs?

The only Mets pitcher with postseason experience is Bartolo Colón, but the 42-year-old is far from the Mets’ best pitcher. Their actual best pitcher, Jacob deGrom, is only in his second season as a big league pitcher. Additionally, he receives the difficult assignment of taking on Kershaw in Game 1. Following him will be rookie Noah Syndergaard and then Matt Harvey. It’s no question that the Mets’ strength lies in their power pitching, but can this young staff pitch as effectively as they did in the regular season? Or have their young arms already been too taxed this year?

Can the Mets keep their offensive surge alive?

The Mets have had one of the best second-half offenses, led by slugger Yoenis Céspedes. But the Mets’ hot hitters will have to face Kershaw in Game 1 and Game 4 (if there is one). And, if they somehow get past Kershaw while firing on all cylinders, they will have to face 19-game winner Zach Greinke in Game 2 and Game 5 (if there is one). Admittedly, that’s a tough task to handle for any team’s offense, but the Cardinals laid the blueprint a year ago. Now it’s up to the Mets to build the house of hits.

My Prediction

This will be the year that Clayton Kershaw displays his regular season dominance in the postseason. It will be a great power-pitching series that the Dodgers will win in five games (3-2), thanks to strong performances by Kershaw and Greinke. If you’re a pitching aficionado, this series will not disappoint in the slightest.

Chicago Cubs vs. St. Louis Cardinals

Busch Stadium, St. Louis, Missouri

Friday, October 9th

Game 1 - 6:45 PM EST on TBS

Do the Cubs know that they’re not supposed to be playing this good just yet? This past Tuesday night, they won their first postseason game (and series, technically) since the infamous “Bartman catch.” The Cardinals, on the other hand, are back in familiar territory, but face the Cubs for the first time in postseason history.

Jon Lester: The good, the bad, or the ugly?

If the Cubs want to win this series, they will need Jon Lester to pitch like he did with the Boston Red Sox when he helped them win World Series titles in 2007 and 2013. He pitched very poorly last year in the AL Wild Card game with the Oakland A’s, yielding six earned runs in 7.1 innings. Will the DirecTV Jon Lester show up in the NLDS this year? Or will the cable version sink the Cubs?

Are the Cards stacked for St. Louis?

Are any of you aware that the Cardinals have never won the World Series with Adam Wainwright, their ace, as a starter??? In 2006, Waino was used as a reliever in the regular season, making 61 appearances, and pitched in nine games in the playoffs. He was used as the closer when Jason Isringhausen got injured. Waino went on the notch four saves to help secure a World Series title. Following the 2006 season, Waino became a starter and the Cardinals didn’t win a title until 2011 – when he underwent Tommy John surgery and didn’t pitch at all that year. The Cards reached the World Series in 2013 with Waino as a starter. He pitched effectively, posting a 2-3 record with a 2.57 ERA that postseason. However, they still fell to the Red Sox. Last year, with Waino as a starter, they lost to the Giants in the NLCS. As fate would have it, Wainwright went down with a ruptured Achilles tendon early this season and is now back to pitch in a relief role. Could this be a sign that the Cards are destined to win this year?

My Prediction

The Cubs have come a far way this season and they will be a formidable foe for the Cardinals. However, the Cards have way too much postseason experience, not to mention home-field advantage. They will win the series in four games (3-1).
