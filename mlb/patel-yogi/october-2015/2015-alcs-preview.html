2015 AL Championship Series Preview

Toronto Blue Jays vs. Kansas City Royals

by Yogi Patel

Share Tweet

Toronto Blue Jays vs. Kansas City Royals

Kauffman Stadium, Kansas City, Missouri

Friday, October 16th

Game 1 - 8:07 PM EST on FOX

What an epic ALDS we witnessed. Both series went to the decisive fifth game and the Royals and Blue Jays both walked away victorious. However, they did so in different ways. The Royals leaned on a strong performance from Johnny Cueto, while they Blue Jays benefitted from the strangest 7th inning you will ever see in any game ever. The diversity of these teams leads into my key questions for this series.

Feast or Famine?

There’s no doubt that the blue birds will get their share of homers, but can they manufacture runs in a more traditional way? Kansas City ranked 9th in the big leagues with 155 homers surrendered. The Jays are 10-45 with RISP. Troy Tulowitzki has been absolutely atrocious – he’s struggling to bat his weight (.095 average) -- and Josh Donaldson’s postseason struggles continue. They’ve been getting great production from Revere, Pillar, and Colabello. But against a pitching staff that is far greater than the Rangers’ staff they just faced, small ball might be a more effective approach. However, if Joey Bats’ bat flip somehow helped the other superstars find their strokes, the Royals are in for some trouble.

Will David Price finally turn the corner?

Clayton Kershaw finally turned the corner a couple of nights ago when he pitched 7 strong innings on short rest. Will David Price finally be able to do the same and get his first victory as a starter? This staff desperately needs him. Marco Estrada, the game one starter, and Marcus Stroman were the only two effective pitchers in the ALDS. The Jays lost Brett Cecil as well, so their bullpen is in dire need of reinforcements.

Imagine if you had a monster like Price coming out of that ‘pen instead of struggling as a starter. It reminds me of Tim Lincecum for the Giants back in 2012. They rode Matt Cain and Madison Bumgarner to a World Series title. It should be noted that the Giants had a much better pitching staff. However, I don’t think this would be a bad option for the Jays, but how would you possibly explain making a blockbuster trade mid-season to bring in an ace like David Price for crucial games like these and then send him to the bullpen? Price simply needs to step up and turn the corner.

Can the Royals continue their magic?

There’s no question about how determined these guys are. They desperately want to finish what they started last year. Johnny Cueto was phenomenal in, arguably, the most important start of his career. They are getting timely, clutch hits and it seems like there’s no deficit these guys can’t overcome. That’s not necessarily a good thing. Having to overcome big deficits means that their starters aren’t pitching as well as they should. Speaking of those starters, who are led by Cueto and Yordano Ventura, they need to carry the momentum from Cueto’s last start and pitch between 6-7 effective innings and then turn it over to the their lockdown bullpen. If they can do that, they can (and will) win. Good pitching always beats good hitting. Especially in the postseason.

My prediction

With the struggles of Tulo and Donaldson, the Jays have to rely heavily on their other boppers. However, the Royals struggled with the Houston Astros, and the Blue Jays have a better offense than the ‘Stros. But the Blue Jays do have a glaring weakness in their pitching staff and the Royals have a better lineup than the Rangers.

If you successfully followed that twisting-turning logic of mine, then you know that I have to pick the Royals because of their dominant bullpen and ability to overcome deficits with timely hits. Royals win in six games.
