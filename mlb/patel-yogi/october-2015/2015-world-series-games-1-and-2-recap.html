2015 World Series - Games 1 and 2 Recap

The series heads into a pivotal three game stretch in New York

by Yogi Patel

Share Tweet

Kansas City Royals vs. New York Mets

Citi Field, Flushing, New York

Friday, October 30th

Game 3 - 8:07 PM EST on FOX

I can’t think of a better way to start the World Series than a 14-inning affair filled with drama and suspense. Edinson Volquez started that game despite the tragic death of his father only a few hours earlier in the Dominican Republic. According to Ned Yost and the Kansas City Royals, the family asked the team not to tell Volquez because they wanted him to make the start.

The Royals’ Alcides Escobar started the game off with a bang. He mashed a lead-off inside-the-park home run off of the first pitch thrown by the Mets’ Matt Harvey. Other than that, the Dark Knight was solid until the sixth inning in his World Series debut. Volquez held his own as well, despite giving up three runs in six innings. However, as I mentioned in my World Series preview, these Kansas City Royals’ hitters will work the counts, and wait you out. That is exactly what they did to Matt Harvey. Harvey only recorded two punch-outs, but limited the walks to two. But the Royals played small ball in the sixth inning and were able to score two runs, including the tying run, on a Hosmer sacrifice fly and a single by the Moose.

But the Mets did not fold. In the 8th inning, Wilmer Flores reached on a rare error by Hosmer, which led to Juan Lagares scoring the go-ahead run. Jeurys Familia entered the game in the 8th inning with two outs. He successfully got the third out of the inning and came back out for the 9th inning with the Mets holding a 4-3 lead. He retired the first batter he faced. Then came Alex Gordon. Familia left one up in the zone on a 1-1 count, and Gordon drove the ball 430 feet to tie the game. Everyone watching the game knew that the Mets would lose from that point on because Familia, their best bullpen pitcher, was 5/5 in save opportunities this postseason and now even he’d run into some trouble.

And to everyone’s surprise, the Mets held on until the 14th inning, when Eric Hosmer was given the chance to redeem himself after the costly error in the 8th. He came to the plate with the bases loaded and no one out. Bartolo Colón was on the mound for the Mets. Colón had pitched two innings of solid relief to this point. However, Hosmer would be the last man he faced. Hosmer drove a ball deep to right field that scored Escobar from third to end the game. It was sad to see such a thrilling Game 1 come to an end.

Royals’ Defense

The Kansas City Royals are the best defensive team in the AL. The Mets are a great defensive club as well. However, both teams had defensives miscues that led to runs being scored in Games 1 and 2. Eric Hosmer made a costly error in the 8th inning of Game 1 that led to the Mets scoring the go-ahead run. Again, in Game 2, Mike Moustakas’s throw caused Hosmer to lose the bag in the 4th inning, which led to Yoenis Céspedes scoring the game’s first run.

Mets’ Defense

Even though the Mets also have a great defensive team, they have also run into their fair share of blunders and mishaps. For example, in the bottom of the 14th in Game 1, David Wright made a throwing error that led to Escobar reaching first to lead off the inning. Then, as mentioned earlier, Escobar was able to advance to third before Hosmer drove him in for the game’s winning run. For the Mets’ defense, the key to winning this series will be to limit the Royals hitters’ on-base percentage and a surefire way to accomplish this is to start by first limiting those defensive errors.

Stellar Pitching in Game 1

Both starters pitched great in Game 1. The relievers pitched great, too. But more credit should go to Bartolo Colón and Chris Young. Colón tossed 2.1 innings in relief, but gave up the game-winning run. Young tossed 3 innings of no-hit ball and recorded four strikeouts. Not to mention, Young will be starting Game 4. Both of these heavyweights, and the rest of the bullpen pitchers, deserve applause.

Alex Gordon

There’s no denying that Gordon is the leader on this team. He showed that when he came to the plate against a dominant closer in Familia. Familia didn’t make an outright terrible pitch during Gordon’s at-bat and this is why it helped that Gordon was just ready to crush any small mistake, and subsequently help his team win the game.

Johnny Cueto

The polarizing Cueto was filthy at home in Game 2. He fired a complete game two-hitter and gave up only one run while recording four strikeouts and three walks. He became the first pitcher to throw a complete game two-hitter in the World Series since Greg Maddux did in 1995. Will we see this type of performance again from him if there is a Game 6?

Royals’ Hitters

I’ve said it in my World Series preview and earlier in this column. But I’ll say it again. These hitters on the Royals will work the count and wait you out. Jacob deGrom threw 94 pitches in five innings and only fanned two hitters. Early in the game, he looked much better than he did in his previous three starts this postseason. However, he was not able to put hitters away.

The Royals’ hitters kept fouling off pitch after pitch until deGrom threw something they could drive somewhere. Hopefully Noah “Thor” Syndergaard will be able to change that luck when they resume play later today at Citi Field.
