A Clash for the Ages in Chi-Town

The Cubs host the Nationals in an exceedingly satisfying four-game showdown

by Yogi Patel

Share Tweet

The NBA Playoffs are well underway, with the usual suspects – the Spurs, Warriors, and Cavaliers – doing their damage. Though baseball is still many months away from reaching its own postseason, the Chicago Cubs and the Washington Nationals, two teams that expect to be in the postseason, are facing off against each other in an epic best-of-four match. They are two of the hottest teams in baseball, and it shouldn’t come as a surprise to hear that both clubs sit in first place in their respective divisions. They have already played two of the four games, with the Cubbies decidedly winning both of those. Let’s take a look at how these teams have fared so far.

Chicago

These aren’t your father’s Cubs, or your grandfather’s Cubs, for that matter. This HAS to be the best start this franchise has ever gotten off to – no, this HAS to be the best Cubs team ever, already. After the win against the Nationals on Friday afternoon, they are 22-6. That’s an absurd .786 winning percentage! They have yet to lose back-to-back games and they are absolutely crushing everyone in the NL Central (13-2 record). They have a 13-3 record away from Wrigley Field.

Oh, and they also have a run differential of +98, which puts them on pace for a historic record. The 1995 Cleveland Indians ended their season with a run differential of +233. And that was a team that featured players you may have heard of over the intertwining years: Jim Thome, Albert Belle, Manny Ramirez, and Kenny Lofton. In 1998, the New York Yankees ended the season with a run differential of +309. They went on to win 114 games and the World Series that year. In 2001, the Seattle Mariners finished with a +300 run differential and won 116 games.

The Cubs are on pace to shatter all of those records by a large margin. And that’s not even the most surprising part about this hot start.

This Cubs offense still isn’t firing on all cylinders.

As a team, they rank first in runs scored (164), second in on-base percentage (.363), ninth in slugging percentage (.426), and tenth in batting average (.258). However, Anthony Rizzo hasn’t hit as well as he can – yet he’s still slugging .644 with ten homers. Jason Heyward hasn’t hit his stride – he’s only hitting .211. Jorge Soler (.182/.282/.288) failed to take over left field duties after Kyle Schwarber was injured. If Dexter Fowler, an early season MVP candidate, can keep up his hot start (.337/.455/.561), Kris Bryant avoids the sophomore slump, and Tommy La Stella keeps on raking, this will be one historic offense backed by a dangerous rotation.

Washington

Fueled by an epic failure a year ago, these Washington Nationals are playing with a chip on their shoulder. They know that the New York Mets are the team to beat in the National League East. However, the Nats are sitting in first place with a 19-10 record. The stellar record is inflated in part because they’ve played a fairly easy schedule thus far; however, expect them to be tested at Wrigley Field this weekend.

Despite dropping the first two games of this four-game series, they are a very solid team. Reigning NL MVP Bryce Harper has hit a dry spell as of late. In his last fifteen games, spanning forty-nine at-bats, Harper is hitting .204/.361/.429 with only three homers and nineteen strikeouts. More surprisingly, Max Scherzer has struggled mightily this year. The Cubs beat him up, scoring seven runs and hitting four homers in just five innings off of him on Friday afternoon. For the season, Scherzer sports a 3-2 record with a 4.60 ERA.

Luckily for the Nationals, Gio Gonzalez (1.15 ERA), Joe Ross (1.23 ERA), and Tanner Roark (2.35 ERA) are amongst the top ten in NL ERA. Stephen Strasburg (2.36 ERA) ranks eleventh. If their starters can keep this pace up, their ace, Scherzer, should straighten things out in the interim. Daniel Murphy can definitely continue to hit at this .400 level, and when Bryce Harper starts hitting again, which he should due to his below average .246 BABIP, this team will be one of the toughest teams to beat in the National League.

As for their weekend series, the Cubs won the first two games.

On Thursday night, Joe Ross faced off against Kyle Hendricks. Ross was very effective, limiting the Cubs potent offense to only two runs in 6.2 innings. He fanned nine while only walking two. However, Ben Zobrist spoiled the young hurler’s night in the fourth inning when he hit a two-RBI single to right field. Again in the eighth inning, Zobrist put the game out of reach when he hit a two-run home run to left-center field off of Felipe Rivero. The Nationals showed some life late in the game when Jayson Werth hit a two-run shot of his own in the ninth inning off of Travis Wood. The Cubs easily won the game 5-2.

Friday afternoon at Wrigley Field was a day designed for hitters. It was windy and the wind was blowing towards the outfield. Wrigley Field was a launching pad during this game. The fun began in the first inning when Anthony Rendon hit a solo shot to left-center off of John Lackey to give Max Scherzer a quick 1-0 lead. Then in the second inning, Scherzer helped himself by notching a RBI single to give the Nats a 2-0 lead. But everything unfolded for Scherzer after that. La Stella hit a long two-run homer in the second inning to give John Lackey and the Cubs new life. Then in the third inning, Rizzo and Zobrist launched back-to-back homers, both to right field. Zobrist followed up his monster shot with a three-run shot in the fifth inning to make the score 7-2. Fowler tagged on an additional run in the sixth inning by hitting a RBI single off of Blake Treinen. Again, the Nationals showed signs of life late when they scored two runs in the eighth off of Clayton Richard, and two more runs off of Justin Grimm in the ninth. However, the Nationals were never truly in the game after the fifth inning. The final score, 8-6 Cubs, might reflect a close game but the Cubs won this game easily.

On Saturday afternoon, the Nationals will send Gio Gonzalez to the mound to face Jason Hammel. Gonzalez is 2-1 with a 1.15 ERA. Hammel is 4-0 with a 1.24 ERA.

On Sunday, Tanner Roark will have a showdown against the reigning NL Cy Young award winner, Jake Arrieta.

While the Cubs have easily beaten the Nationals, what they should be the happiest about is that a man named Murphy isn’t beating them…again.
