2016 MLB Season Predictions

The birds are chirping, the grass is pristine, it must be baseball season!

by Yogi Patel

Share Tweet

Spring Training is underway, and before you know it, Opening Day will be here. Who will be this year’s version of the 2015 Mets or Cubs? Can the Cardinals stay atop the stacked NL Central? How will the Dodgers fare without co-ace Zack Greinke? Will David Price stabilize the Red Sox’s rotation? There are plenty of questions that all of us are ready to see answered. But for now, here are my Spring Training predictions for the 2016 season. Enjoy!

NL East

Only a year removed from asking where his ring was after the Nationals signed ace Max Scherzer, Bryce Harper has gone on the record and anointed the Mets as the team to beat this year. Harper backed up his words last year, winning the NL MVP honors after having a monster season (.330, 42 HR, 99 RBI, 9.9 WAR). As for the rest of the Nats, they just struggled to stay healthy. Ian Desmond, who signed with the Texas Rangers just a few days ago, had an atrocious season. Meanwhile, the Dark Knight, the deGrominator, Thor, and the ageless wonder, Bartolo Colón, led the Mets to their first division title since 2006 and their first World Series appearance since 2000, when Roger Clemens famously threw Mike Piazza’s bat back at him.

However, we always know that the division is won on the field, over the course of a 162-game season. That’s why I think the Nats will win the NL East. The Mets’ young hurlers will take a step back after they each threw more innings than they had before. Yes, they expect to get Zach Wheeler back around June and could switch to a 6-man rotation to give their young arms a breather. Offensively, they are expecting the same Yoenis Céspedes that surged them to the title during the second half of last season. On the other hand, the Nationals just need to stay healthy. Stephen Strasburg is entering his walk year, so expect him to deliver his finest season en route to his team’s third NL East crown in the last five years.

Prediction: Washington Nationals

NL Central

The Cubs are the clear favorites. However, John Mozeliak always has a “Card” up sleeve. The Redbirds are at their best whenever the odds are stacked against them. This year will be no different. It will be a close race and I believe the Cubs will be the second-best team in the National League. However, a rotation that consists of Adam Wainwright, Carlos Martinez, and Michael Wacha is about as solid of as they come. St. Louis Manager Mike Matheny will find a way to get the best out of players, such as Brandon Moss and Matt “Big City” Adams. Matt Holliday, entering the final year of his contract, should stay healthy, which will result in a productive final season. Young sluggers Stephen Piscotty and Randal Grichuk will prove that last year was not a fluke. Expect both young phenoms to surpass the 20-homer mark this year. Tommy Pham will be a valuable asset off the bench and as a fourth outfielder, if injuries derail Holliday.

Prediction: St. Louis Cardinals

NL West

World Series titles in 2010...2012...2014!

Thankfully, 2016 is divisible by two. If the trend continues, we shouldn’t be surprised to see Bruce Bochy and Buster Posey getting their fourth ring. However, I’m not picking them because just because it’s 2016; I’m picking them because of their stacked rotation. They piled up on aces and are hoping Matt Cain can find the same magic before injuries took their toll. Newcomers Jeff Samardzija and Johnny Cueto should fare well in a much larger ballpark, like AT&T Park, and coming back to the pitcher friendly NL will be the cherry on top. Add them with ace Madison Bumgarner and you have a very solid rotation with a ton of depth.

Their offense is just as stacked. Adding leadoff hitter and solid center fielder Denard Span really stabilizes their rotation. The Dodgers have won the division the past five years, but they enter this year with way too many question marks in the rotation behind Clayton Kershaw and in their lineup. Ultimately, the NL West title will remain in Golden State, but I expect the Bay Area team to clinch it when the time arrives.

Prediction: San Francisco Giants

NL Wild Cards

Both the Mets and the Cubs are baseball’s Cinderella stories in the making, but those $160+ million and $130+ million payrolls will help create any happy ending they dream up. The Mets have the best rotation in all of baseball and the Cubs have the best offense in all of baseball. They were hard to pick as my wild-card teams due to their insuppressible firepower in all aspects, but it would be fun to see both teams featuring in a single wild-card game for all the marbles.

Prediction: New York Mets and Chicago Cubs

AL East

Let me be blunt: This is a terrible division with no clear alpha-male of the pack. The Red Sox added their long-time nemesis David Price to the rotation while also acquiring the best closer in all of baseball, Craig Kimbrel. The Yankees had a relatively quiet offseason. Their only big move was trading for Aroldis Chapman. They have a very solid and stable bullpen. Their starters only need to go six effective innings and their offense has to be able to score runs. If they can do that, game over.

The Rays could get in the mix and fight for the division crown. As for the O’s, I see them finishing second. As for the Blue Jays, the only noise they’ll make is trading the expensive José Bautista.

Prediction: New York Yankees

AL Central

2015 World Series winners. Best bullpen in the majors. They’re still the team to beat.

Prediction: Kansas City Royals

AL West

The Texas Rangers have a staff that’s led by Yu Darvish (scheduled to make his first start in May) and Cole Hamels, the prized gem of the rotation. The ageless Adrián Beltré is coming off of yet another solid season and Prince Fielder should be poised for an even greater output season this year. Their signing of Ian Desmond will make up for the lack of production from maligned slugger Josh Hamilton. They also have plenty of talent in the minors, in case anyone gets hurt. They’re the deepest team in the West and boast a very strong rotation.

Prediction: Texas Rangers

AL Wild Cards

I’m bullish on the Tigers and the Astros here. The Tigers made a few big moves in this offseason, bring aboard Justin Upton and Jordan Zimmermann. Justin Verlander appeared as if he had regained his Cy Young form in the second half of last season. This is a very solid team they’ve put together.

The Astros shored up the back end of their bullpen with Ken Giles. They also have a rising superstar in Carlos Correa to go along with a stellar rotation. They tasted the postseason last year, and they’ll be hungry for more this year.

Prediction: Detroit Tigers and Houston Astros

NL MVP

Prediction: Anthony Rizzo

Rizzo has been somewhat overlooked and underrated throughout his career. But this year, I think he’ll have a season to remember. In a Cubbies offense already featuring stars like Kris Bryant, Addison Russell, Kyle Schwarber, Jorge Soler, and Jason Heyward, I expect Rizzo to outshine them all on his way to a monster season (37 HR, 128 RBI), which should earn him the NL MVP honors.

Honorable Mentions: Kris Bryant, Bryce Harper, Buster Posey, Paul Goldschmidt

AL MVP

Prediction: Mookie Betts

Betts belted 18 homers and drove in 77 RBIs while hitting .291/.341/.479 last year. That was good enough to compile a 6.0 WAR. I expect him build on those numbers and have a career year this year with the Sox.

Honorable Mentions: Mike Trout, Miguel Cabrera, Salvador Perez, Carlos Correa, Manny Machado

NL Cy Young

Prediction: Jacob deGrom

Jacob deGrom has been sensational since entering the big leagues. He’ll continue his success and win the first of his many Cy Young awards. But should the deGrominator slip up, studs like Clayton Kershaw, Jake Arrieta, and Zack Greinke will be right on his heels.

Honorable Mentions: Clayton Kershaw, Zack Greinke, Jake Arrieta, Max Scherzer, Madison Bumgarner

AL Cy Young

Prediction: Chris Sale

Sale is always in the discussion for this award. This year, he’ll find a way to stay healthy and dominate like he has the past two years. The Sox signing David Price garnered much brouhaha, and justifiably so. I expect Price to provide some healthy competition for Sale, and if Price and Sale falter even a tad bit, expect Dallas Keuchel and Cole Hamels to cause some waves.

Honorable Mentions: David Price, Félix Hernández, Cole Hamels, Dallas Keuchel, Chris Archer

NL Rookie of the Year

Prediction: Corey Seager

He’s the top prospect in baseball and all he did when he was called up last September was hit. He posted a .337/.425/.561 slash-line while adding four homers and 17 RBIs in his 98 at-bats. He will be an everyday player for the Dodgers starting this season, so we can expect gaudy numbers for his rookie campaign.

Honorable Mentions: Steven Matz, Julio Urías, Trea Turner, J.P. Crawford

AL Rookie of the Year

Prediction: José Berríos

The Twins need help in their rotation and they will look internally for help. Berrios will come up and help stabilize that rotation with a solid rookie campaign.

Honorable Mentions: Lewis Brinson, Byung-ho Park, Byron Buxton, Aaron Judge, Blake Snell
