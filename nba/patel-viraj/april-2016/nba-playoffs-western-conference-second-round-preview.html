NBA Playoffs: Western Conference Second Round Preview

Golden State and San Antonio headline a competitive second round

by Viraj Patel

Share Tweet

Although the Heat/Hornets and Raptors/Pacers series remain in a deadlock, the rest of the second round series have been set, and this round’s highly-anticipated Thunder/Spurs series will kick things off tonight in San Antonio. Thus far, the Western Conference has given us more superstar injuries – Stephen Curry, Chris Paul, and Blake Griffin have all fallen for the time being – than thrilling games.

Conversely, the Eastern Conference has provided one surprise after another. Only two out of ESPN’s 21 esteemed NBA writers believed Cleveland would sweep the Pistons, a feat the Cavaliers pulled off before the ink had dried on Stan Van Gundy’s offseason rap album contract. Only Ethan Sherwood Strauss believed the Pacers had a chance to advance past the second-seeded Raptors, yet the Raptors have been 1-4 in closeout games – add one more to the loss column after their Game Six loss in Indiana – and have “never won a best-of-seven postseason series.” Oh, and aside from some scintillating basketball, the Heat/Hornets series gave us the infamous “Purple Shirt Man,” and the Hawks/Celtics series gave rise to Isaiah Thomas’s otherworldly abilities in both scoring and slapping.

What did the West give us?

Just sadness. Dave Joerger’s many tears, Austin Rivers’s many stitches, and ESPN’s many “Steph-Curry-is-down-but-is-he-out” Facebook posts topped the list in a painful first round in the west. But starting tonight, expect all of that to change. The Thunder visit San Antonio tonight – as does Charles Barkley and the TNT gang (we all know where this is headed, right?) – and that series is set to become the second round’s finest. So without further ado, let’s build off of what Matt Dengler accomplished in the first round and preview the Western Conference’s second round series.

No. 2 San Antonio Spurs vs. No. 3 Oklahoma City Thunder

Series begins on: Today, April 30, 2016 in San Antonio at 8:30 PM Eastern on TNT

Regular season head-to-head record: Both teams won two games, with the home team winning each time.

Prediction: Despite the Thunder challenging at each turn, the Spurs take the series in Game Six in Oklahoma City.

No. 2 San Antonio Spurs

Overarching storyline: San Antonio boasts the second-best team in the NBA, but will that be enough to quickly dispose OKC? If the series stretches out through all seven games, the Spurs will have no time to heal before the Warriors come calling.

One player to watch: Kawhi Leonard (and who Kawhi Leonard covers defensively) will be the most intriguing storyline of this series. The two-time Defensive Player of the Year will probably be asked to guard Kevin Durant; however, if Gregg Popovich sees an exploitable opportunity, he may stick Kawhi on Russell (neutralizing Russ) and hand Durant over to Danny Green or Kyle Anderson.

Team health: The Spurs will field a completely healthy roster.

The mind-boggling stat: According to Andrew Flowers and Neil Paine of FiveThirtyEight, “During the regular season, when Leonard matched up against Durant on defense, KD scored 38 points per 100 team possessions; but when Kawhi guarded Westbrook, he held him to only 25 points per 100 team possessions. If Westbrook faces Danny Green or Tony Parker instead, his scoring rate rises to 48 and 33 per 100 team possessions, respectively.”

Bandwagon potential: Sky-high, because in a world where the 73-9 Golden State Warriors did not exist, this team would easily be the best. The Spurs also play total team basketball, working synchronously on both offense and defense.

The coach factor: Gregg Popovich is the GOAT. Need I say more?

Difference maker off the bench: This was a close tie between David West and Kyle Anderson, with the nod going to David West. If Pop places Kawhi on Westbrook defensively, then Anderson’s coverage of Durant will be instrumental for the Spurs. When David West enters the game, the Spurs will get yet another tough body to field against Serge Ibaka, who has to be kept limited since Russ and KD will be looking to explode in every game.

They’ll lose this series if: Westbrook plays his heart out, Durant posts eye-popping stat lines, and Ibaka and Kanter explode for a game or two.

No. 3 Oklahoma City Thunder

Overarching storyline: What jersey the Thunder will wear. Just kidding, just kidding (although Russell Westbrook seems to make the uniform decisions in OKC). The actual overarching storyline surrounds the missile that is Russell Westbrook. Can he stay dominant with a Tony Parker or Danny Green on him? Will he be neutralized if Popovich pulls a Popovich and throws Kawhi on him? Will the uniform picking duties distract him?

One player to watch: Serge Ibaka could potentially shift this series in OKC’s favor. Expect both Durant and Westbrook to have one huge game apiece, granting the Thunder two wins. But the third and fourth wins will be a collective effort made possible by guys like Serge Ibaka and Enes Kanter playing the games of their lives.

Team health: The Thunder will field a completely healthy roster.

The mind-boggling stat: This is far and away the toughest defense the Thunder will face in this postseason. In fact, Kevin Durant and Russell Westbrook have never faced a defense more dominant than that of the 2015-16 Spurs, according to the aforementioned Andrew Flowers and Neil Paine of FiveThirtyEight. All the more reason for Ibaka and Kanter to show up locked and loaded.

Bandwagon potential: High, because the Thunder have two widely recognized superstars, and most new NBA fans will flock to Durant and Westbrook (and, as a result, their team) as the series progresses.

The coach factor: Oh boy. Despite his success at the collegiate level, Billy Donovan will be overmatched by the Spurs’ curmudgeon-in-chief, Gregg Popovich. It’s not even close.

Difference maker off the bench: Enes Kanter was my choice for the Sixth Man award (which went to Jamal Crawford of the Clippers). The seven-footer will come off the Thunder bench in this series, and if he’s exploding offensively and contributing with his impressive offensive rebounding, expect the Thunder to capitalize on all those second chances. The Thunder led the NBA in offensive rebounding in the regular season while the Spurs languished in the bottom tiers, so this is one of the few exploitable areas for Billy Donovan’s side.

They’ll lose this series if: Russ gets neutralized, KD is minimized, and the Spurs just adhere to their excellent offensive and defensive performances.

No. 1 Golden State Warriors vs. No. 5 Portland Trail Blazers

Series begins on: Sunday, May 1, 2016 in Oakland at 3:30 PM Eastern on ABC

Regular season head-to-head record: The Warriors won three out of four.

Prediction: With or without Stephen Curry, I foresee the Warriors clinching the series in five games.

No. 1 Golden State Warriors

Overarching storyline: With Stephen Curry’s injury, can the Warriors speed past a tough Blazers side?

One player to watch: Klay Thompson could have a field day on C.J. McCollum’s lackadaisical defense (-0.27 real plus/minus). If C.J. can’t contain Klay, expect Klay to go bananas in the Bay.

Team health: Only Stephen Curry remains sidelined with injury. The reigning MVP is expected to return by (or before) May 9, per ESPN.

The mind-boggling stat: In 78 regular season games this season, Shaun Livingston averaged 19.5 minutes per game, 6.3 PPG, 2.2 RPG, and 3.0 APG. In the five postseason games against the Rockets, Livingston averaged 27.0 minutes, 13.2 PPG, 3.6 RPG, and 4.8 APG. So with more playing time, Livingston improved his stat line rapidly and doubled his scoring. What levels will he reach if this continues? Will these results be enough to knock of the Blazers and give an ailing Curry the entire series off?

Bandwagon potential: Gargantuan, mostly because ESPN has decided to hoist the Dubs as the world’s best team, a title they mostly deserve. The Dubs fans have always been loyal to their squad, but the legions of bandwagoners will look to join the Warriors crusade with each and every game. Be geared for even more non-stop love from the Warriors’ number #1 fan, ESPN.

The coach factor: Steve Kerr began this year’s postseason with Coach of the Year honors. But in this series, he faces Terry Stotts, who was one of Kerr’s prime challengers for the honor and can coach on pace with the best of the West. How often will Kerr deploy the Curry-less “Lineup of Death” in this series? Will he get outfoxed by Stotts?

Difference maker off the bench: Though he’s starting in place of Stephen Curry at the moment, Shaun Livingston can potentially shut down Damian Lillard with his length.

They’ll lose this series if: Stephen Curry remains sidelined for a prolonged time and Damian Lillard and C.J. McCollum prove too much to handle for the Dubs defense.

No. 5 Portland Trail Blazers

Overarching storyline: Although the Blazers only defeated the Dubs only once this regular season, they know they can knock down the defending champions. But that statement victory in Portland took a Herculean effort from Damian Lillard, who shot lights-out in a career-high 51-point performance.

One player to watch: C.J. McCollum will play the Robin to Lillard’s Batman, but the Most Improved Player award winner will have to channel his inner Batman at times throughout this series if the Blazers want to compete.

Team health: The Blazers will field a completely healthy roster.

The mind-boggling stat: C.J. McCollum appeared in all six of Portland’s highest-scoring lineups in the regular season (whereas Lillard only appeared in three of those), which means he shepherds the second unit when Lillard sits. If McCollum can corral that second unit to produce as it had done in the regular season, the short-handed Warriors may struggle.

Bandwagon potential: Medium, since Lillard is a true superstar that bandwagoners can flock to if he has another scoring explosion against the Warriors. But the Trail Blazers have long appealed to a Northwest corner of the country while the rest of the nation went mad for the Chicago Bulls, New York Knicks, and the Los Angeles Lakers (all teams who didn’t even make the playoffs, I might add), so bandwagoning should remain limited with Portland.

The coach factor: Terry Stotts has become a coaching guru with Portland. Stotts’ contributions took Portland from everyone’s lottery rankings in the preseason to everyone’s title contenders list this postseason. That magic should continue in this tough second round matchup with the Warriors. Stotts has to make sure that the Blazers don’t get killed defensively, and he’ll have to coax the most out of his two stars in Lillard and McCollum.

Difference maker off the bench: Ed Davis improved so much this season that even Zach Lowe was clamoring for the big man out of North Carolina to receive Sixth Man considerations. Lowe added, “Davis has better stats across the board: 61 percent from the floor as a basket vulture, the fourth-best offensive rebounding rate in the league and much better rim-protection numbers.” If Ed Davis contains Draymond Green, then some of the Blazers’ defensive worries will subside, and Phys Ed’s offensive rebounding abilities should set up key second chances for Portland.

They’ll lose this series if: Lillard and McCollum fail to capitalize offensively and, instead, get harassed on defense by Livingston and Klay Thompson.
