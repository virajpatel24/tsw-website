Welcome to The Sports Whorl

The start of something new.

by Viraj Patel

Share Tweet

At the end of this September, seventeen months will have passed since when I first made the mental (you can apply the “crazy” definition of “mental” here if you’d like) decision to write more. The months that have followed since May 2014 have allowed me to transform that mental decision into physical proof. From that incredibly spur-of-the-moment decision has come something beautiful.

And while I can continue on that rewarding path for decades and decades, I’ve come to a fork in the road. I can either continue going solo or I can collaborate with other like-minded individuals to collectively produce some of the best (written) work of our lives. I choose the latter.

Welcome to The Sports Whorl.

“Life will deliver a few moments when something substantial is about to happen, when you know it’s substantial, when you’ve done everything you could to prepare for the moment, but still, you just don’t know. And it would be foolish to pretend otherwise. Oh my God. There is no stopping this now. Please tell me this will turn out all right. You take a leap of faith with life. You inhale and exhale. You hope.”

— Bill Simmons in his debut article for Grantland

I’m starting The Sports Whorl as a long-form sports publication. We will have a rotating staff of contributors. I’ll head up the main editing duties and oversee the English Premier League portion of the site. I’m excited for what I’ll learn in the process.

But I am even more excited for the team of columnists and editors we have. I am proud to present The Sports Whorl team:

1. Satya Polisetti – Satya will head up the NBA section of The Sports Whorl and his deep knowledge for all things NBA will provide the necessary fuel for that section to thrive. Also, if you love LeBron James, then Satya’s work will be your go-to source for all things LeBron.

2. Yogi Patel – Yogi will be in charge of the MLB and the NFL content. He is about as knowledgeable as they come when it comes to baseball, and his NFL knowledge runs just as deep. The MLB and NFL portions of the site will be in great hands with him in charge.

3. Ryan Dean – Ryan and I “met” after trading some thoughts on some sports articles I wrote a few months back. He is one of the most promising writers we have to offer and, in due time, he’ll be one of those can’t-miss people you’ll want to read. If you don’t believe me, then check out his debut article about Leicester City FC’s rise to the English Premiership throne. Ryan will be a columnist for the English Premier League section of the site.

4. Nate Preval – Nate and I go back a long way. We met in high school and the friendship has continued since then. Nate will serve as the columnist for the English Premier League. Since the 2015 Rugby World Cup is going on right now, Nate has agreed to provide exclusive coverage for The Sports Whorl. Nate has written an excellent debut article that I highly recommend you check out: Crouch. Bind. Set!

5. Teerth Brahmbhatt – Teerth’s a great friend and is one of the most versatile people I know. In every single conversation we have, I always come away learning something new. As a reader of his work, that has to be an enticing draw and I'm sure he'll impress us all with his upcoming articles. His deep knowledge of the Chicago sports landscape and the NBA will undoubtedly inform his columns for The Sports Whorl.

Again, can’t wait to show you what all of these guys are capable of – they’ve already wowed me with the bits and pieces of their work that I have seen. And, they’re only going to get better. That’s the main goal with this publication: To improve as writers. If we stay true to that vision and goal, we will all be on our way to creating a publication you love reading.

That progression and improvement will take us time. And none of us are probably ready for this – you never are for a step this big. I’m certainly not. But when doubts start to creep in (and they did multiple times in the process of creating this website even), I always remind myself of this: “An individual in a creative field will only succeed when they’re truly unafraid to fail.”

So here’s to a lot of future failures, some successes, and a ton of learning. We’ll do our very best and put in the work. That’s all I can promise. Enjoy the site.

Viraj Patel
