Super Bowl 50 Recap

Backed by a dominant defense, Peyton Manning and the Denver Broncos triumphed in Santa Clara

by Yogi Patel

Share Tweet

We witnessed an epic defensive showdown in Super Bowl 50. Both defenses played lights out for the duration of the game, but the Denver Broncos’ defense came out on top, limiting the high-powered Carolina offense to only ten points.

Defense wins Championships

As I mentioned in my Super Bowl 50 preview last week, the Broncos defense would have to contain MVP quarterback Cam Newton. They did just that. The Broncos front seven, led by DeMarcus Ware and Von Miller, terrorized the Panthers offensive line and sacked Newton six times on Sunday. Von Miller, MVP of Super Bowl 50, was responsible for 2.5 sacks and two forced fumbles, which led to the two touchdowns that the Broncos scored. DeMarcus Ware was equally effective as he registered two sacks of his own and four total hits on Newton.

This elite defense was able to force Newton into plenty of throwing situations. They held the Panthers to just 118 rushing yards (45 yards for Newton, 29 yards for Jonathan Stewart, 26 yards for Fozzy Whittaker, and 18 yards for Mike Tolbert) on 27 total carries (6 by Newton, 12 by Stewart, 4 by Whittaker, and 5 by Tolbert) and one touchdown (Stewart). By containing the Panthers’ dangerous ground attack, they forced Cam Newton to try and beat them with his arm. Newton, lacking reliable receivers around him, struggled right from the beginning, only completing 18 of his 41 attempts. In his first Super Bowl appearance, he would throw no touchdowns and register a lone pick. This is a Carolina Panther team that relies heavily on the ground game, and by taking that away, the Broncos turned the Panthers into a one-dimensional team that could be easily beat.

SuperCam’s Kryptonite

Cam Newton had a rough night in Santa Clara last Sunday. His numbers fail to tell the complete story. The one pick he threw was not really his fault. That pass hit Ted Ginn right in his hands, as the Panthers were marching deep into Broncos territory. Jerricho Cotchery dropped two pivotal passes. One of those drops was up the sideline and would have easily been a touchdown, which could have altered the game. Cotchery also had a catch that was controversially ruled as an incomplete pass in the first quarter. Coach Ron Rivera challenged the call, but the play stood as called.

Newton was under pressure the entire game. Miller, Ware, and the rest of the front seven terrorized the Panthers offensive line. Anytime the Panthers were gaining momentum on offense, the offensive line would get called for a false start, which made it impossible for Cam and J-Stew to get into any sort of a rhythm. You could easily tell that the Broncos defensive line got into the heads of the Panthers offensive line. Newton could not get anything going with the limited time he had in the pocket and, when that’s combined with the evident lack of quality receivers on the team, it makes it difficult for even Cam Newton to flourish.

A Country for Old Men

Peyton Manning had an ugly game himself. He finished the night completing 13 of his 23 passes for 141 yards. He passed for zero touchdowns, and continued his streak of throwing an interception in his every Super Bowl appearance. His one fumble and six sacks also made things dicey for Denver. He also got away with some horrendous passes that the Panthers should have picked off, including two dropped picks by star cornerback Josh Norman. At one point, Manning was even strip-sacked twice on the same drive, but Carolina only recovered the second fumble.

Despite the overall lackluster performance, Manning got off to a great start. He led a 64-yard opening drive, which ended in a field goal to start off the game. However, Manning was not able to do much else after that. He had eleven drives that failed to generate even ten yards, and seven of them ended in three-and-outs. Denver combined to total just 45 yards from scrimmage. They only scored one offensive touchdown, which was a C.J. Anderson rushing touchdown. The other touchdown they scored was a Malik Jackson fumble recovery in the endzone. The Broncos offensive unit ended the night with just one third down conversion on fourteen attempts. Essentially, Manning and the offense were bailed out BIG time by their elite defense.

In the end, that Denver Broncos defense easily contained the Carolina Panthers elite offense, granting Manning his second Super Bowl ring. However, the road back to the Super Bowl next season is much easier for the Carolina Panthers, who can rely upon Cam Newton and that defense to strap into a second gear en route to yet another deep run in the 2016-17 season. As for Peyton Manning, we will have to wait for him to finish drinking his Budweiser before we know if he will be returning for another rodeo.
