NFL Wild Card Preview

Eight teams face off this weekend, only four will advance

by Yogi Patel

Share Tweet

Kansas City Chiefs vs. Houston Texans

NRG Stadium, Houston, Texas

Saturday, January 9, 2016

4:35 PM EST on ESPN, ABC

What a journey it has been for both teams. The Chiefs started off 1-5 before ripping off 10 straight wins to end the season. Houston took advantage of a weak division, some free publicity thanks to HBO’s Hard Knocks, and the mighty struggles of the Indianapolis Colts.

The last time the Chiefs won a playoff game, a fellow by the name of Joe Montana was the quarterback. Alex Smith has done a great job protecting the ball and giving his team every opportunity to win. He ended the season with 20 TDs and seven picks. And his 95.4 quarterback rating is tenth-best in the NFL.

Bill O’Brien’s Texans made the playoffs despite having four different QBs start a game. However, Brian Hoyer has done a great job stepping into the starting role and rejuvenated his team down the stretch.

Both teams took a big hit when their featured running backs went down (Jamaal Charles for the Chiefs and Arian Foster for the Texans). Despite these crucial injuries, the Chiefs had Charcandrick West step up and the Texans had Alfred Blue move into the lead role. However, you have to credit each team’s defense for their success down the stretch. It’s impossible to guard both Jadeveon Clowney and J.J. Watt. If you somehow escape past the Clowney-Watt brigade, you have to face down Brian Cushing’s physicality at LB. If they want to advance to the next round, the Texans will need Clowney to be ready for this weekend (questionable designation).

This game will come down to which team can establish a consistent running game first. Kansas City will go into Houston and walk away with a 27-20 victory, a repeat of their opening week’s performance.

Pittsburgh Steelers vs. Cincinnati Bengals

Paul Brown Stadium, Cincinnati, Ohio

Saturday, January 9, 2016

8:15 PM EST on CBS

The Bengals have lost seven straight playoff games and haven’t won the AFC Championship since 1988. AJ McCarron, a two-time National Champion at Alabama, will get the start against the Steelers this weekend in place of the injured Andy Dalton. Pittsburgh, led by the capable Big Ben, has had a long tradition of winning in the playoffs and will be looking to continue that train of success.

The Steelers have a very explosive offense, despite losing star running back Le’Veon Bell early in the season. DeAngelo Williams did a great job filling in for Bell, but he also finds himself unlikely to play with a right leg injury. While the Steelers’ running game may encounter a few issues tomorrow, their receiving core of Martavis Bryant and Antonio Brown will give Big Ben two dangerous targets that can score at any given time.

At the college level, AJ McCarron had a track record of winning when the lights shined the brightest. But can he translate that same success over to the NFL? He has an explosive offense, too. AJ Green is as good of a receiver as Pittsburg’s Antonio Brown, and McCarron has a huge target in tight end Tyler Eifert. If Jeremy Hill and Giovani Bernard can successfully find gaps, this game could get interesting. However, the Steelers’ postseason experience outweighs that of Cincinnati’s. And Pittsburgh has won four out of the last five meetings between the two teams. Steelers win this one 34-24.

Seattle Seahawks vs. Minnesota Vikings

TCF Bank Stadium, Minneapolis, Minnesota

Sunday, January 10, 2016

1:05 PM EST on NBC

Pete Carroll’s Seahawks struggled mightily coming out of the gate, but Russell Wilson turned that around quickly. He has been playing lights out as of late, and he’s overshadowing how dominant their defense has been doing the stretch. This has easily been Wilson’s best statistical season in his four-year career. I mean 34 TDs, 8 INTs, 4,024 passing yards, 68.1% completion rate, and a QB rating of 110.1? Simply put, Russell Wilson has played like a MVP down the stretch, throwing for 24 TDs compared to just one pick in his last seven games. He also produced a league-best 132.8 passer rating while completing 72.0 percent of his passes in that time frame. Play at the quarterback position doesn’t get much better than that.

The Vikings, led by Adrian Peterson, will have a tough task attempting to establish a strong running game against the Seahawks’ front seven. But Peterson’s league-leading 1,485 rushing yards imply that he’s a formidable threat to any defense. However, the Seahawks won’t be scared of Peterson since they will benefit from getting a healthy Marshawn Lynch back. We already know what to expect from their defense. If Lynch comes back and plays to the level we expect him to play, and if Wilson can continue this dominant stretch, the Seahawks could be a very tough sixth seed.

The Vikings’ defense has played substantially better since being embarrassed by the Seahawks in a lopsided 38-7 loss in Week 13. In that game, Peterson was held to only 18 yards on eight carries. The key for success for the Vikings has always been to run the ball. In games that they’ve rushed for more than 113 yards, they’re 10-0. When they’re held under 113 rushing yards, they’re 1-5. Against the Seahawks, the Vikings need to establish the running game if they hope to win.

With the way the Seahawks defense has played lately (11 forced turnovers in the last six games), this might be the most lethal team in the NFC. Peterson will find more gaps, but will be held well under 113 rushing yards. Seahawks win 24-7.

Green Bay Packers vs. Washington Redskins

FedExField, Landover, Maryland

Sunday, January 10, 2016

4:40 PM EST on FOX

The Packers are hoping the postseason gives them a chance to hit the reset button and start over. The Packers struggled down the stretch. Eddie Lacy hasn’t been the same as last year and Aaron Rodgers sorely misses Jordy Nelson. Washington, on the other hand, decided to go with Kirk Cousins over RGIII as their starter this year. And he rewarded them with the NFC East Title. However, their division was very weak this year.

Rodgers will need Lacy and James Stark to help him get more time in the pocket as he works to find receivers Randall Cobb and Davante Adams. Defensively, the Packers should be able to contain Kirk Cousins. They rank sixth in the NFL against the pass, with 227.6 yards allowed per game.

The Redskins have a lot of work to do if they want to win. They’ll need to find some sort of running game. Their injuries on the defensive side of the ball don’t help matter much, either. Cousins may have to step up and play like a MVP, if he wants to win this game. He’ll need the full support of his receiving targets, most notably Jordan Reed (87 receptions, 952 yards, 11 TDs), Pierre Garcon (72 receptions, 777 yards, 6 TDs), and DeSean Jackson (30 receptions, 528 yards, 4 TDs).

With the injury concerns the Redskins have and the fact that they’re going up against a motivated and fueled Aaron Rodgers, I can’t imagine them winning their first playoff game since December 24th, 1972. The Packers win 28-17.
