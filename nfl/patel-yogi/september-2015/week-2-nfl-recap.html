Week 2 NFL Recap

Unlucky Andrew Luck and Sleeping Seattle Seahawks

by Yogi Patel

Share Tweet

Best Game of the Week

What an epic week for football! In Week 2, we saw the Denver Broncos score two TDs in the final 2 and a half minutes of the first half to tie the game against the Kansas City Chiefs at 14. The epic battle concluded with Denver’s Bradley Roby returning a fumble for 21-yards for a TD to give the Broncos a 31-24 win.

Blowouts

While that game was on the high end of the epic games spectrum, we saw a few blowouts on Sunday. The Steelers just crushed the 49ers, 43-18, and the Cardinals demolished the Bears, 48-23. The Bears suffered an even bigger blow when Jay Cutler left with a hamstring injury and the team later found out he would have to miss a couple of weeks. Or, it’s just a blessing in disguise – time for Chicago to ring in the Jimmy Clausen era! How could that possibly turn out badly?

Take that NFL!

PHOTOGRAPH BY WWW.BOSTON.CBSLOCAL.COM

Throughout his 15-year career, Tom Brady has never been off to a start like he has through the first two games this season. He’s thrown for 754 yards, 7 TDs, zero interceptions, and completed 69.2% of his passes. He has a QBR of 80.8 and a rating of 119.9. Simply put: He’s letting the league know not to mess with him again. If he keeps tearing teams apart like this in coming games, he will be well on his way to yet another MVP award.

Early Season Concerns

Mile High Panic

While the Broncos did beat the Chiefs, I was surprised by how poorly Peyton Manning has played so far this season. He has only thrown 431 yards, 3 TDs, and, to make matters worse, he has already has thrown 2 interceptions. In terms of pass completion (usually a staple of his iconic seasons), he has only completed 58.8% of his passes. His Total QBR sits at a measly 41.0, which is the 4th worst in the league, and his rating is a measly 74.2. If his performance was just this horrific for the first two games of the season, I would not be concerned. However, his performance has been bad since the end of last season, including the playoffs. I really hope Manning can turn it around and be more like the Peyton Manning we all are used to seeing.

Running out of Luck?

PHOTOGRAPH BY BLEACHER REPORT

Another QB that is off to a slow start is Andrew Luck. So far, Luck has thrown 493 yards and 3 TDs, but 5 INTs so far. His Total QBR is 28.5, which is the 3rd worst in the league, and his rating is a weak 58.9. Granted he has gone up against two of the best defenses in the league (Bills and Jets), the Colts just have not clicked on offense.

Andre Johnson (7 receptions for 51 yards) is still struggling to find a rhythm in the system. T.Y. Hilton hurt his knee in the game against the Bills, and then again on Monday night against the Jets. However, Donte Moncrief has made the most of his chances. He has 13 receptions for 168 yards and 2 TDs. The Colts have also struggled to find a consistent running game. They brought in Frank Gore hoping that he would give them the ability to run the ball. However, he only has 88 yards on 23 carries. I think things will start to click for this offense pretty soon. They have their divisional rivals, the Titans, this upcoming week. I think Luck will be happy to face a much softer secondary and will finally get in the win column in Week 3.

Sleepless in Seattle

Reinforcements are coming for the Seahawks. Kam Chancellor is finally ending his holdout. It comes at a much-needed time for the Seahawks. They started the season 0-2 and have not looked the same at all on defense. Not only is Chancellor a big time playmaker for that defense, but he is also a leader. While the defense will get a big time boost, the offense needs to improve as well. Jimmy Graham has not been involved too much. We are talking about the second best TE (Gronk is the best) in the NFL! He only has 7 receptions for 62 yards and one TD. He needs to be more of a focal point for the Russell Wilson led Seahawks offense. Speaking of Wilson, he needs to get Graham and Marshawn Lynch involved more in the offense. Beast Mode has also been fairly quiet thus far. He only has 114 yards on 33 touches. Lynch can open up the play-action passes, and Graham can go and grab the ball. The Seahawks host the turmoiled Bears in Week 3, so I know they will get their first victory of the 2015 season.
